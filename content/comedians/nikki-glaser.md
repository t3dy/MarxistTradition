<!-- Note: Gender performance as commodity; the sexual confession as marketized authenticity -->

Nikki Glaser's comedy exemplifies how sexual confession functions as commodity form in contemporary stand-up. Her material converts intimate experience -- desire, insecurity, bodily anxiety, relationship dynamics -- into marketable content, performing the operation by which capital extracts exchange-value from the most private domains of human life. The more revealing the confession, the higher its value in the attention economy.

This confessional mode is gendered in specific ways. Women comedians face a market that particularly rewards sexual self-disclosure, creating an economic incentive structure that mirrors and reinforces the broader cultural demand for women to perform availability and transparency. Glaser's commercial success is inseparable from her willingness to submit her sexuality to public consumption -- a dynamic that reproduces, in comedic form, the reification of women's bodies under capitalism.

The roast format, where Glaser has achieved particular prominence, concentrates these dynamics. The celebrity roast is a spectacle of mutual commodification in which performers exchange insults calibrated for maximum viral circulation. Each joke is a content unit designed for social media extraction, and the performer's labor consists of converting interpersonal aggression into shareable product. That Glaser excels in this format speaks to her skill; that the format exists speaks to capital's appetite for converting all human interaction into commodity.
