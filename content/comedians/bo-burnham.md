<!-- Note: Inside as alienation under digital capitalism; the performer trapped in the commodity form; meta-comedy as ideology critique -->

Bo Burnham's work, particularly his special *Inside*, constitutes one of the most precise dramatizations of alienation under digital capitalism in contemporary culture. Trapped alone in a room producing content, Burnham literalizes the condition of the digital laborer whose living activity becomes indistinguishable from the commodity form. The performer, the performance, and the product collapse into a single reified object.

His meta-comedic approach -- comedy about the impossibility of comedy, performance about the violence of performance -- operates as ideology critique from within. Burnham does not stand outside the spectacle to criticize it but shows how thoroughly the spectacle has colonized interiority itself. The anxiety, depression, and paralysis he performs are not personal pathologies but symptoms of a mode of production that demands the total subsumption of human subjectivity.

The contradiction is that this critique circulates as a Netflix commodity, generating surplus value for the very platform economy it anatomizes. Burnham is aware of this, and the awareness itself becomes content -- an infinite regress that mirrors capital's capacity to absorb all opposition.
