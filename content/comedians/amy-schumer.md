<!-- Note: Liberal feminism as commodity form; the limits of representational politics in comedy -->

Amy Schumer's comedy represents liberal feminism in its commodity form. Her material addresses real contradictions in gender relations -- sexual double standards, workplace inequality, body image -- but frames them as problems of representation and individual empowerment rather than structural features of capitalist social reproduction. The solution implied is always more inclusion within existing structures, never transformation of those structures themselves.

Her rise coincided with feminism's recuperation as a marketable brand identity in the 2010s. Schumer became a commodity whose use-value was precisely her feminist content, packaged for consumption by an audience seeking progressive identification without political commitment. The trajectory from transgressive stand-up to Hollywood films to brand partnerships traces the familiar arc of rebellion absorbed by capital.

This is not a personal failing but a structural one. Comedy distributed through corporate media necessarily operates within ideological limits set by that media's class interests. Schumer's work illuminates how capitalist culture industries can metabolize critique into content, transforming feminist politics into a lifestyle commodity.
