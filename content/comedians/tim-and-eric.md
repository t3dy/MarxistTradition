Tim Heidecker and Eric Wareheim's work — particularly *Tim and Eric Awesome Show, Great Job!* (2007-2010) — constitutes the most radical formal critique of commercial television in American comedy. Their method is essentially detournement: appropriating the visual grammar of infomercials, local TV, and advertising to expose the grotesque absurdity concealed within these familiar commodity forms. The result is profoundly uncomfortable viewing that reveals the alienation embedded in commercial culture.

Their aesthetic of deliberate amateurism, uncomfortable close-ups, and uncanny repetition makes visible what polished production conceals: the fundamental weirdness of a society in which every human interaction is mediated by advertising and commerce. The infomercial — capitalism's purest cultural form, in which all content serves the selling function — becomes in their hands a vehicle for exposing the commodity form's colonisation of everyday life. That this work was broadcast on Adult Swim, a subsidiary of WarnerMedia, illustrates the culture industry's capacity to commodify even its own critique, though Tim and Eric's work retains a residual resistance in its refusal to provide the pleasure that commodified comedy normally delivers.
