<!-- Note: The total depoliticization of late night; celebrity worship as ideology; entertainment capital's demand for frictionless content -->

Jimmy Fallon's Tonight Show represents the total depoliticization of late night television, and this depoliticization is itself deeply political. By replacing commentary with celebrity games, musical performances, and viral-ready segments, Fallon created a show that functions as pure ideology in Barthes's sense: it presents the existing order as natural, unquestionable, and above all entertaining. The absence of political content is the political content.

Fallon's signature affect -- undiscriminating enthusiasm, delighted laughter at everything, childlike wonder at celebrity -- performs an ideological function. It models the ideal consumer subject: one who greets every commodity with equal excitement, who never discriminates, judges, or resists. The famous hair-tousling of Donald Trump during the 2016 campaign was not a lapse but a logical expression of this posture. For capital, all content is equivalent; all guests deserve the same enthusiastic reception.

The show's optimization for social media virality completed the subsumption of late night under platform capitalism. Each segment is designed as a discrete commodity unit for digital circulation, and the host becomes a brand rather than a person -- a smiling interface between celebrity and audience through which capital flows without friction.
