Nathan Fielder's work constitutes the most rigorous critique of entrepreneurial ideology in contemporary comedy. *Nathan For You* systematically exposed the absurdity beneath small business capitalism's self-presentation. By offering real business owners increasingly preposterous strategies -- strategies that nevertheless follow the internal logic of marketing and disruption to their conclusions -- Fielder revealed that the rationality of entrepreneurial capitalism is indistinguishable from insanity when its premises are taken seriously.

The show's cringe comedy operates through the affect of alienation. The discomfort the audience feels watching Fielder's interactions with real people is the experience of social relations reduced to their instrumental core -- human connection stripped of everything except its utility for capital accumulation. This is what Lukacs described as reification made visible: social relations appearing as relations between things.

*The Rehearsal* extended this analysis to its logical extreme. The attempt to eliminate contingency from human interaction by rehearsing every possible scenario is a perfect metaphor for capital's drive to subsume all of life under its rationality. The result is not mastery but deeper alienation -- each rehearsal produces new uncertainties, each attempt at control generates new chaos, mirroring capital's own tendency to produce the crises it seeks to prevent.
