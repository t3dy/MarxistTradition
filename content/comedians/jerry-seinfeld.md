Jerry Seinfeld's comedy about nothing is, from a Marxist perspective, comedy about everything -- which is to say, comedy about the commodity form as it saturates everyday life. The observational style transforms the mundane rituals of consumer existence into material for exchange, performing exactly the operation Marx identified: the conversion of use-value into exchange-value. Every social interaction, every minor frustration, every trip to the store becomes raw material for the comedic commodity.

The show *Seinfeld* elevated this to a formal principle. The no-hugging-no-learning rule ensured that no episode would disturb the characters' relation to their world, producing a universe of pure stasis where social relations are natural and permanent. This is reification as sitcom structure -- the world as it is, unchangeable, available only for ironic commentary rather than transformation.

Seinfeld's public support for Israel's military campaigns, including visiting troops during the assault on Gaza, reveals the political content concealed within the apolitical stance. The comedian who claims to be above politics consistently aligns with imperial power -- a reminder that the refusal of politics is itself a political position, one that invariably serves the ruling class.
