The Israeli assault on Gaza constitutes a systematic campaign of settler-colonial genocide, rooted in the structural logic of Zionism as a project of territorial accumulation through the dispossession and elimination of the indigenous Palestinian population. This process follows the historical pattern identified by Marx in his analysis of primitive accumulation, whereby the violent separation of people from their land and means of subsistence is the foundational act of capitalist property relations. The destruction of Gaza's infrastructure, housing, hospitals, and agricultural land is not collateral damage but the deliberate annihilation of the material conditions necessary for Palestinian social reproduction.

The role of the United States as the primary financial and military patron of Israel reveals the class interests at stake. Billions of dollars in annual military aid constitute a direct transfer of public funds to the American arms industry, which profits from the testing and deployment of its weapons systems on a captive Palestinian population. This circuit of capital, from US taxpayers to arms manufacturers to the Israeli military to the rubble of Gaza, exemplifies the intimate connection between imperialism, militarism, and capitalist accumulation that Luxemburg identified as essential to the reproduction of capital on a world scale.

The ideological management of this genocide within Western liberal democracies exposes the limits of bourgeois humanitarianism. The same states that proclaim universal human rights provide the bombs, diplomatic cover, and media frameworks that enable mass killing. The selective application of international law reveals its function as an instrument of imperial power rather than a genuine constraint on state violence. A Marxist analysis insists that the liberation of Palestine is inseparable from the global struggle against imperialism and capitalism, as the structures that produce colonial violence in Gaza are the same structures that exploit and oppress working people everywhere.
