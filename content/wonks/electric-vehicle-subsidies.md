Electric vehicle subsidies — tax credits, purchase rebates, and manufacturer incentives — are promoted as essential for climate transition. The US Inflation Reduction Act provides up to $7,500 per vehicle in consumer tax credits, while billions flow to manufacturers for battery plant construction.

Marxist analysis reveals the class character of EV policy. Subsidies disproportionately benefit higher-income consumers who can afford new vehicles, while working-class communities remain dependent on aging petrol cars and underfunded public transit. Massive public subsidies to Tesla, GM, and other corporations socialise the investment risk while privatising profits. The focus on individual car ownership as the solution to transportation emissions preserves the automobile-dependent spatial organisation of capitalism rather than investing in public transit, cycling infrastructure, and walkable urban design that would serve broader class interests and achieve deeper emissions reductions.
