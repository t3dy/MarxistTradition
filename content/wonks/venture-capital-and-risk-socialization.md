Venture capital's self-image as the engine of innovation conceals a systematic pattern of risk socialisation and profit privatisation. The foundational technologies behind the most successful VC-backed companies — the internet, GPS, touchscreens, Siri's voice recognition, the mRNA platform behind COVID vaccines — were developed with public research funding through DARPA, NIH, NSF, and university labs.

VC enters after the riskiest basic research has been publicly funded, captures the commercialisation gains, and extracts enormous returns through IPOs and acquisitions. The model further socialises risk through tax subsidies: carried interest is taxed at capital gains rates rather than income rates, and startup losses are deductible. Mazzucato's work on the 'entrepreneurial state' demonstrates that the public sector bears the greatest innovation risks while the private sector captures the rewards. A Marxist response demands public equity stakes in publicly funded innovations, ensuring that the returns on collective investment flow back to the public rather than to Sand Hill Road.
