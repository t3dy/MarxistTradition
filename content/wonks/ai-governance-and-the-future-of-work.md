AI governance debates concern the regulation of artificial intelligence systems as they transform labour markets, concentrate economic power, and reshape social relations. Mainstream policy discussion centres on algorithmic bias, job displacement, and safety regulation, but Marxist analysis situates these within capitalism's fundamental dynamics.

From a Marxist perspective, AI governance debates miss the structural issue: under capitalism, AI serves capital accumulation. Automation eliminates jobs not because technology is autonomous but because capitalist firms deploy it to reduce labour costs and increase profits. Platform monopolies like Google and Amazon use AI to extract rents from data and control market access. Policy proposals for retraining, universal basic income, or algorithmic auditing treat symptoms while leaving intact the class relations that determine who owns and benefits from technological development.
