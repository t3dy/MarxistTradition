*The Origin of German Tragic Drama* (Ursprung des deutschen Trauerspiels, 1928) was Benjamin's Habilitation thesis, rejected by the University of Frankfurt and subsequently published as a book. The work analyses the German Baroque mourning play (Trauerspiel) — a minor literary genre — to develop a revolutionary theory of allegory, history, and criticism.

Benjamin distinguishes allegory from the Romantic concept of the symbol: while the symbol claims immediate unity of meaning and appearance, allegory presents meaning as ruined, fragmentary, and historically contingent. The Baroque allegorist confronts a world drained of transcendent meaning, where history appears as natural catastrophe and sovereign power reveals its arbitrary, violent foundations. The methodological prologue, the 'Epistemo-Critical Prologue,' outlines Benjamin's theory of philosophical presentation through 'constellations' and 'ideas' — a method that would later inform his analysis of modernity in *The Arcades Project*. The work's difficulty contributed to its academic rejection but established concepts central to Benjamin's later thought.
