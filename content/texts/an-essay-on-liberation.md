*An Essay on Liberation* (1969) was written in the wake of the May 1968 uprisings and represents Marcuse's most optimistic statement about revolutionary possibility in advanced industrial society. The essay argues that the technical achievements of capitalism have created the material preconditions for a qualitatively different civilisation based on a 'new sensibility' — an aesthetic and erotic relationship to nature and other human beings.

Marcuse contended that liberation requires not only the transformation of economic structures but a revolution in human needs and instincts themselves, proposing a 'biological foundation for socialism.' He identified the student movement, Third World liberation struggles, and the counterculture as bearers of this new sensibility. The text remains influential in debates about the politics of desire, the role of aesthetics in revolutionary transformation, and whether capitalism can be challenged through the creation of alternative forms of life.
