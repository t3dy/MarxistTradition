*Studies on Authority and the Family* (Studien über Autorität und Familie, 1936) was the first major collaborative research publication of the Frankfurt School's Institute for Social Research. Edited by Horkheimer, with contributions from Fromm, Marcuse, and others, it investigated how the patriarchal family produces character structures predisposed to authoritarian submission.

The study combined theoretical essays with empirical surveys of working-class and middle-class families in several European countries. Fromm's contribution analysed how the decline of the father's economic authority under monopoly capitalism paradoxically intensified authoritarian tendencies, as insecure family structures produced individuals seeking compensatory domination. The research provided the foundation for the later *Authoritarian Personality* studies and established the Frankfurt School's distinctive method of integrating Marxist social theory, Freudian psychoanalysis, and empirical social research.
