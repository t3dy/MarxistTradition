A Companion to Marx's Capital provides a chapter-by-chapter guide to Volume 1, drawing on Harvey's decades of teaching Capital in university seminars and public lectures. Harvey emphasizes the internal logic of Marx's argument while connecting it to contemporary patterns of urbanization, financialization, and crisis. The book has become one of the most widely used introductory guides to Marx's critique of political economy.
