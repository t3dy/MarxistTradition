*Prisms* (1955) is a collection of Adorno's cultural criticism essays, including the influential 'Cultural Criticism and Society,' which contains his famous and frequently misunderstood dictum about the barbarism of writing poetry after Auschwitz. The essays exemplify Adorno's method of immanent critique — reading cultural objects not from an external standpoint but by tracing the contradictions within their own logic.

The collection includes studies of Spengler, Huxley, Kafka, Veblen, and the sociology of knowledge, demonstrating Adorno's range across literature, philosophy, and social science. Each essay analyses how cultural phenomena simultaneously reveal and conceal the social contradictions from which they emerge. Adorno's method treats cultural criticism itself as entangled in the culture it criticises — the critic cannot claim a position outside the totality. This reflexive approach to ideology critique has influenced subsequent cultural theory, from Jameson's analysis of postmodernism to contemporary debates about the politics of aesthetics.
