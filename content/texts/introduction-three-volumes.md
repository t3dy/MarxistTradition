Heinrich's *Introduction* presents *Capital* from the perspective of the *neue Marx-Lekture* (new reading of Marx), a German-language tradition of Marx scholarship that emphasizes the monetary dimension of value theory and rejects substantialist interpretations of abstract labor as a physiological or energetic magnitude. Heinrich distinguishes Marx's critique of political economy from both classical political economy and what he calls "worldview Marxism" — the codified doctrine of orthodox Marxist parties — arguing that Marx's project was not to construct an alternative economics but to demonstrate the historically specific and socially constituted character of economic categories under capitalism.

The book covers all three volumes of *Capital* in a compact format, making it one of the few introductory texts to treat the work as a unified theoretical project rather than isolating Volume I. Heinrich is particularly incisive on the monetary theory of value, arguing that value can only exist in and through money as its necessary form of appearance, and on the distinction between Marx's critique and Ricardo's labor theory of value. He also offers a careful reconstruction of the tendency of the rate of profit to fall, arguing that Marx's own formulation contains logical difficulties that have not been adequately resolved.

Since its translation into English in 2012, the *Introduction* has become a key reference point for value-form theoretical approaches to Marx across the Anglophone world, particularly among scholars seeking alternatives to both traditional Marxist economics and post-Marxist cultural theory.
