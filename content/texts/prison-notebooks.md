The Prison Notebooks comprise Gramsci's fragmentary reflections on politics, culture, and philosophy, written during his imprisonment under Italian fascism and published posthumously between 1948 and 1951. Gramsci's central contribution is the concept of hegemony, which analyzes how ruling classes maintain power not only through coercion but through the organization of consent in civil society, education, and common sense. The Notebooks have exerted enormous influence across political theory, cultural studies, and postcolonial thought.
