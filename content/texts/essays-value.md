Rubin's Essays interprets Marx's value theory as fundamentally a theory of social relations between people mediated by things, foregrounding the qualitative analysis of the value-form over quantitative determination. Written in the Soviet Union during the 1920s, the work challenged productivist readings of Marx by insisting that abstract labor is a historically specific social category, not a physiological substance. Rubin's approach was suppressed under Stalinism but became a major influence on the value-form tradition after its rediscovery in the 1970s.
