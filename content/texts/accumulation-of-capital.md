The Accumulation of Capital argues that capitalist expanded reproduction is impossible within a closed system and that capital necessarily requires non-capitalist social formations as markets for surplus production, driving the process of imperialism. Luxemburg's critique of Marx's reproduction schemas in Volume 2 of Capital generated lasting debate about the dynamics of accumulation and crisis theory. The work remains a foundational text in Marxist theories of imperialism and global capitalism.
