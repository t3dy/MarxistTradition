Mute Compulsion develops a theory of the specifically economic power of capital, arguing that capitalism subordinates human life not primarily through ideology or direct violence but through impersonal structural mechanisms rooted in market dependence. Mau draws on Marx's mature critique of political economy to show how capital reshapes the conditions of social reproduction, producing a form of domination that operates silently through the compulsion of economic relations. The book has been widely discussed as a significant contribution to contemporary Marxist theory.
