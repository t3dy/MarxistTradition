The Grundrisse consists of Marx's preparatory notebooks for Capital, first published in 1939, containing his most extensive reflections on dialectical method and the architecture of his critique of political economy. The manuscripts range across the theory of money, the general concept of capital, and speculative passages on automation and post-capitalist society that have no parallel in the published volumes. Its rediscovery reshaped debates about Marx's intellectual development and the relationship between Hegelian categories and economic analysis.
