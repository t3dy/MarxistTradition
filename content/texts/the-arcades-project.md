*The Arcades Project* (Das Passagen-Werk) is Walter Benjamin's massive, unfinished work on nineteenth-century Paris as the capital of modernity, compiled between 1927 and his death in 1940. Organised as a montage of quotations and commentary across dozens of thematic 'convolutes,' it examines the Paris arcades, panoramas, world exhibitions, fashion, prostitution, Haussmannisation, and the figure of the flâneur as expressions of commodity capitalism's dreamworld.

Benjamin developed the concept of the 'dialectical image' — a constellation in which past and present are brought into critical contact, enabling the flash of revolutionary recognition. The commodity's phantasmagoric character creates collective dream-images that must be awakened from rather than interpreted. Harvey's *Paris, Capital of Modernity* engages extensively with Benjamin's project. The work's fragmentary, non-linear form has been read as both its limitation and its methodological innovation, embodying a critical practice adequate to capitalism's own contradictory totality.
