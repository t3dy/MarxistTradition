Capital, Volume 1 presents Marx's systematic critique of political economy, beginning with the analysis of the commodity form and developing through the theories of value, money, surplus-value, and the general law of capitalist accumulation. The work demonstrates how capital as a social relation reproduces itself through the exploitation of labor-power, tracing the historical process of primitive accumulation that separated producers from the means of production. It remains the foundational text for all subsequent Marxist economic analysis and critique.
