*Reading Capital* originated in a seminar at the Ecole Normale Superieure in Paris during the 1964-65 academic year and introduced the method of symptomatic reading to Marxist theory. Althusser argues that Marx's mature work operates with a concept of structural causality irreducible to either Hegelian expressivism — in which every part of the social whole expresses a single essence — or empiricist models of knowledge that treat theory as a passive reflection of given facts. Instead, Marx's *Capital* produces its object theoretically, constructing the concept of the mode of production as a complex, structured whole in which causality operates through the articulation of relatively autonomous instances (economic, political, ideological) rather than through simple determination.

Althusser and his collaborators contend that *Capital* enacts an epistemological break from the ideological problematic of the early Marx to the scientific problematic of the mature critique of political economy. This thesis generated intense debate about continuity and discontinuity in Marx's thought, the status of humanism and historicism in Marxist theory, and the relationship between science and ideology.

The complete Verso edition of 2015 restores the contributions of Ranciere, Macherey, and Establet that were omitted from earlier English translations, revealing the collective and internally contested character of the project. Ranciere's contribution, which he later disavowed, offers a critique of the concept of critique itself, while Macherey's analysis of Marx's relationship to Hegel remains underappreciated. *Reading Capital* is essential for understanding structuralist Marxism, the Althusserian legacy, and the philosophical stakes of reading Marx's economic writings.
