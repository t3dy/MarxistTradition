The Communist Manifesto outlines the materialist conception of history as a succession of class struggles and analyzes the revolutionary role of the bourgeoisie in dissolving feudal social relations while producing its own gravedigger in the proletariat. Written as a programmatic document for the Communist League, it combines historical analysis with a call for the abolition of private property and the self-emancipation of the working class. It is the most widely read and translated text in the Marxist tradition.
