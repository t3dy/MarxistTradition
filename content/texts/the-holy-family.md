*The Holy Family, or Critique of Critical Criticism* (1845) is the first jointly authored work by Marx and Engels, a polemical attack on Bruno Bauer and the Young Hegelians of the *Allgemeine Literatur-Zeitung*. The title satirises Bauer and his brothers as a 'holy family' of speculative philosophy that substitutes abstract criticism for practical engagement with social reality.

Marx and Engels argue that the Young Hegelians' 'critical criticism' remains imprisoned within Hegelian idealism, opposing 'Spirit' to the 'Mass' and treating real social movements with philosophical contempt. Against this, Marx develops a materialist position grounding critique in the actual conditions of the proletariat and the real movement of civil society. The text contains early formulations of historical materialism, including the argument that the proletariat is compelled by its material conditions to abolish private property. While often dismissed as an ephemeral polemic, the work marks a crucial stage in Marx and Engels's break with German philosophical idealism and their turn toward materialist social analysis.
