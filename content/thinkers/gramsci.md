Antonio Gramsci developed the concept of hegemony to explain how ruling classes maintain power not only through coercion but through the production of consent within civil society, including education, media, and cultural institutions. Writing from a fascist prison between 1929 and 1935, his *Prison Notebooks* rethought the relationship between structure and superstructure, the role of intellectuals in class formation, and the strategic distinction between wars of position and wars of maneuver. His work became a central reference for Western Marxism, cultural studies, and post-colonial theory from the 1960s onward.
