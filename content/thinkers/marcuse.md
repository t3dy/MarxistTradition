Herbert Marcuse was a German-American philosopher and critical theorist who became one of the most influential intellectual figures of the 1960s New Left. In One-Dimensional Man, he argued that advanced industrial society had developed sophisticated mechanisms of social control that absorbed opposition and flattened critical thought into conformity. His work combined Freudian psychoanalysis with Marxist social theory, proposing that genuine liberation required not only economic transformation but also the emancipation of instinctual life from repressive civilization.
