Mark Fisher was a British cultural theorist and blogger whose Capitalist Realism became one of the most widely read works of Marxist cultural criticism of the twenty-first century. The work's central thesis — that it is easier to imagine the end of the world than the end of capitalism — captured the ideological closure of the post-2008 moment, in which the system's manifest failure produced not alternatives but a deepened sense of inevitability. Fisher's concept of 'capitalist realism' describes not a belief that capitalism is good but the pervasive sense that no alternative is possible. His blogging at k-punk pioneered a form of cultural criticism that moved fluidly between music, film, politics, and theory, and his writing on depression as a political rather than personal condition connected mental health to the structural violence of neoliberalism.
