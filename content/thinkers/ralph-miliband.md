Ralph Miliband was a Belgian-born British political sociologist and Marxist theorist whose work on the capitalist state made him one of the most important political thinkers of the postwar British left. A professor at the London School of Economics and later at Leeds University, he founded the *Socialist Register* annual in 1964 and edited it for three decades.

In *The State in Capitalist Society* (1969), Miliband argued against pluralist political science by demonstrating that the state in advanced capitalist countries systematically serves the interests of the capitalist class, not because of conspiracy but through the social origins of state personnel, the structural dependence of the state on capital, and the ideological hegemony of the dominant class. His debate with Nicos Poulantzas over whether the state should be analyzed instrumentally or structurally became one of the defining theoretical exchanges in Marxist political theory. Miliband's insistence on combining empirical investigation with theoretical analysis of state power remains influential in Marxist political sociology.
