Ruha Benjamin is an American sociologist whose Race After Technology introduced the concept of the 'New Jim Code' to describe how automated systems — from predictive policing algorithms to healthcare risk assessments — encode and reproduce racial discrimination under the guise of technological neutrality. Her work demonstrates that algorithmic bias is not a technical glitch to be debugged but a structural feature of systems designed within and for a racially stratified society. Benjamin's contribution connects the Marxist analysis of technology as embodying specific social relations to critical race theory's insistence that those relations are fundamentally racialised, showing that the automation of decision-making automates existing patterns of domination.
