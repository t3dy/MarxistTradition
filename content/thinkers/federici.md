Silvia Federici is an Italian-American scholar and activist whose work has fundamentally reshaped Marxist understandings of gender, reproduction, and primitive accumulation. In Caliban and the Witch, she argued that the transition to capitalism required the violent subjugation of women and the enclosure of reproductive labor, reinterpreting the European witch hunts as a constitutive moment of capitalist accumulation. Her earlier agitation in the Wages for Housework campaign brought international attention to the unwaged domestic labor that sustains capitalist production yet remains invisible within orthodox political economy.
