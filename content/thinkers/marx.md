Karl Marx developed a systematic critique of capitalist political economy centered on the labor theory of value, the analysis of surplus value extraction, and the commodity form. His method of historical materialism provided a framework for understanding social formations as structured by their mode of production and the class relations arising from it. His later work, particularly the three volumes of *Capital* and the preparatory *Grundrisse*, remains the foundational reference point for all subsequent Marxist theory.
