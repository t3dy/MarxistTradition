Nicos Poulantzas was a Greek-French political sociologist who developed one of the most influential Marxist theories of the capitalist state. Drawing on Althusser's structuralism, he argued that the state possesses relative autonomy from the dominant class, functioning to organize the long-term interests of the power bloc while disorganizing the working class. His later work moved toward a relational theory of the state as a condensation of class forces, influencing subsequent debates on state theory, authoritarianism, and democratic socialism.
