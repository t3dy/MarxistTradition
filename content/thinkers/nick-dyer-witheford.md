Nick Dyer-Witheford is a Canadian political theorist whose work applies autonomist Marxist frameworks to the analysis of digital capitalism. His Cyber-Marx updated the autonomist tradition for the information age, arguing that struggles over communication technologies represent a new terrain of class conflict. Cyber-Proletariat examined how digital networks simultaneously enable new forms of labour exploitation — from coltan mining to click-work — and new possibilities for resistance, while Inhuman Power (with Atle Mikkola Kjøsen and James Steinhoff) analysed artificial intelligence through the lens of Marx's concept of the general intellect, arguing that AI represents capital's attempt to subsume cognitive labour as fixed capital.
