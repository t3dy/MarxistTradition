David Harvey integrated Marxist political economy with geography, arguing that capitalism resolves its internal crises of overaccumulation through spatial reorganization, a process he termed the "spatial fix." His concept of accumulation by dispossession extended Marx's analysis of primitive accumulation to describe ongoing processes of enclosure, privatization, and dispossession under neoliberalism. His lecture series and companion volumes to *Capital* have introduced Marx's critique of political economy to a broad contemporary audience.
