Benjamin Bratton is an American theorist whose The Stack proposed a comprehensive model of planetary-scale computation as a new form of political geography. The work argues that overlapping layers of digital infrastructure — from undersea cables to cloud platforms to user interfaces — constitute an accidental megastructure that is reshaping sovereignty, territory, and political subjectivity in ways that exceed the frameworks of both liberal political theory and classical Marxism. While not a Marxist in any orthodox sense, Bratton's analysis of how computational infrastructure reorganises the material conditions of production, governance, and social life provides essential raw material for any Marxist theory of digital capitalism.
