Paul Baran was a Russian-born American Marxist economist who taught at Stanford University and was a central figure in the Monthly Review school of Marxist thought. His work focused on the political economy of development and the structural mechanisms by which advanced capitalist countries maintained the underdevelopment of the Third World.

In *The Political Economy of Growth* (1957), Baran introduced the concept of "potential economic surplus" to explain how imperialism, feudal remnants, and monopoly power prevent underdeveloped countries from utilizing their productive capacities. His collaboration with Paul Sweezy produced *Monopoly Capital* (1966), which argued that mature capitalism is characterized by giant corporations that generate a surplus far exceeding profitable investment opportunities, leading to stagnation, waste, and militarism. Baran's work provided the theoretical foundations for dependency theory and for the Monthly Review tradition's analysis of imperialism, monopoly, and economic stagnation.
