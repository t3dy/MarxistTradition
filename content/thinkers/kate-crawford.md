Kate Crawford is an Australian researcher whose Atlas of AI provided a materialist analysis of artificial intelligence that counters the dominant understanding of AI as disembodied computation. The work traces AI's material infrastructure — from lithium mines and underpaid data labourers to the energy consumption of data centres and the environmental costs of hardware production — demonstrating that what is presented as a purely technical system depends on vast chains of extraction, exploitation, and ecological destruction. Crawford's contribution lies in reconnecting the apparently immaterial world of algorithms to the material conditions of its production, showing that AI systems encode and reproduce existing structures of class, racial, and colonial domination.
