Christian Fuchs is an Austrian critical theorist whose work applies Marxian political economy to the analysis of digital media and communication. His Digital Labour and Karl Marx systematically extended Marx's labour theory of value to the digital economy, arguing that social media users perform unpaid labour that generates surplus value for platform corporations. Fuchs draws on the Frankfurt School tradition alongside Marx to analyse how digital capitalism transforms the production, circulation, and consumption of information into sites of exploitation, demonstrating that the apparently free and participatory character of social media conceals the extraction of value from user activity.
