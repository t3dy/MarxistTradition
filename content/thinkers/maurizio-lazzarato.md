Maurizio Lazzarato is an Italian sociologist and philosopher whose early work on 'immaterial labour' — labour that produces informational, cultural, or affective content — was foundational for autonomist analyses of post-Fordist capitalism. His later work shifted focus to debt as the fundamental social relation of neoliberal capitalism. The Making of the Indebted Man argues that debt is not merely an economic mechanism but a technology of governance that produces a specific subjectivity: the indebted subject who internalises obligation, guilt, and responsibility, thereby ensuring compliance with the demands of capital without the need for direct coercion.
