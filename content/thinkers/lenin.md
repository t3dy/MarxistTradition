Vladimir Lenin theorized imperialism as a structural stage of capitalist development characterized by the dominance of finance capital, monopoly, and the territorial division of the world among great powers. His *The State and Revolution* reinterpreted Marx and Engels on the class character of the state and the necessity of its destruction in the revolutionary process. As the principal leader of the October Revolution of 1917, his political and theoretical legacy shaped the trajectory of twentieth-century Marxism worldwide.
