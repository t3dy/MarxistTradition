Jodi Dean is an American political theorist whose concept of 'communicative capitalism' describes how the proliferation of digital communication paradoxically undermines the conditions for effective political action. Blog Theory and related works argue that the internet's promise of democratic participation is ideological: the endless circulation of opinions, takes, and content substitutes for political organisation, producing the feeling of political engagement without its substance. The Communist Horizon and Comrade represent Dean's argument that the left must recover the concepts of party and comrade as organisational forms capable of producing collective political agency against the atomising tendencies of communicative capitalism.
