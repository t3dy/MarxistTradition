Frantz Fanon was a Martinican-born psychiatrist, philosopher, and revolutionary who became one of the most influential theorists of anti-colonial struggle in the twentieth century. In Black Skin, White Masks, he analyzed the psychic damage inflicted by colonialism and racism, drawing on phenomenology and psychoanalysis to describe the alienation of colonized subjects. The Wretched of the Earth, written during the Algerian war of independence, offered a searing account of colonial violence and argued that decolonization necessarily involves a fundamental restructuring of the social order from the ground up.
