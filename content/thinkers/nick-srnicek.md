Nick Srnicek is a Canadian political economist whose Platform Capitalism provided one of the most influential Marxist analyses of the digital economy. The work argues that platforms — from Google and Facebook to Uber and Amazon — represent a new business model in which the extraction of data has become a key source of competitive advantage and profit, constituting a distinct form of capitalist accumulation. His earlier Inventing the Future (with Alex Williams) called for a left politics that embraces technological development, arguing for full automation, universal basic income, and the reduction of the working week as demands that could move beyond capitalism rather than merely ameliorating it.
