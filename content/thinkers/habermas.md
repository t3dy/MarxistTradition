Jürgen Habermas is a German philosopher and sociologist who represents the second generation of the Frankfurt School. His theory of communicative action shifted critical theory away from the pessimism of Adorno and Horkheimer by grounding rationality in the structures of everyday linguistic interaction oriented toward mutual understanding. His work on the public sphere and deliberative democracy has been enormously influential across political philosophy, sociology, and legal theory, arguing that legitimate political norms must emerge from inclusive, reason-based public discourse.
