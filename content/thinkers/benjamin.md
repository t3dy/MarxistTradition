Walter Benjamin was a German literary critic and philosopher whose eclectic body of work combined Marxist materialism with Jewish messianic theology. His essay on mechanical reproduction analyzed how technologies like photography and film transformed the nature of art by destroying its traditional aura of uniqueness and authenticity. His final work, the Theses on the Philosophy of History, offered a radical critique of progressive historicism, insisting that revolution emerges not from historical inevitability but from a redemptive interruption of catastrophic continuity.
