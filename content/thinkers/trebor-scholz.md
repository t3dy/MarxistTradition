Trebor Scholz is a German-American scholar and activist who coined the term 'platform cooperativism' to describe an alternative to the extractive model of platform capitalism. His work argues that the gig economy's apparent innovation — connecting workers with customers through digital platforms — conceals a regression to piece-work and the destruction of labour protections won over a century of struggle. Platform cooperativism proposes that workers and users should own and govern the platforms through which they labour, applying the cooperative tradition to digital infrastructure. Scholz's intervention represents an attempt to move beyond critique toward practical alternatives, though its reliance on market competition with venture-capital-funded platforms raises questions about viability within capitalist conditions.
