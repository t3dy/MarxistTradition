Bertell Ollman is an American political scientist and Marxist theorist known for his work on dialectical methodology and the concept of alienation. A professor at New York University, he has devoted his career to explicating the philosophical foundations of Marx's thought, with particular attention to Marx's method of abstraction and the philosophy of internal relations that underpins it.

In *Alienation: Marx's Conception of Man in Capitalist Society* (1971), Ollman provided a systematic reconstruction of Marx's theory of alienation, showing how it pervades all aspects of life under capitalism. His later work, *Dance of the Dialectic* (2003), offered a detailed account of how Marx employs abstraction, moving between different levels of generality and vantage points to illuminate the interconnected totality of capitalist society. Ollman's contributions have been important for clarifying the philosophical method underlying Marx's critique of political economy and for defending dialectical reasoning against positivist and analytical approaches to social science.
