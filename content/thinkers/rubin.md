Isaak Rubin produced what is widely regarded as the most rigorous analysis of Marx's theory of value prior to the neue Marx-Lekture, emphasizing that value categories are expressions of historically specific social relations of production rather than transhistorical economic quantities. His *Essays on Marx's Theory of Value* foregrounded the value-form and commodity fetishism as central to Marx's critique, rather than treating them as peripheral to a labor-embodied theory of price. Rubin was executed during the Stalinist purges in 1937, and his work was largely suppressed until its rediscovery in the 1970s.
