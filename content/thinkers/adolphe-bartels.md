Adolphe Bartels was a Belgian democratic radical who participated in the revolutionary movements of the 1840s and was active in the Democratic Association of Brussels, where he collaborated with Marx and Engels. He served as the association's vice-president, with Marx as vice-president for the German contingent.

Bartels represented the strand of Belgian radicalism that sought democratic reform through popular mobilisation. His significance lies primarily in the organisational context of Marx's Brussels period (1845-1848), when Marx was developing historical materialism and building international connections among democratic and working-class movements. The Democratic Association brought together Belgian radicals, German émigrés, and Chartist representatives, prefiguring the internationalist organisational forms that Marx would later develop through the International Working Men's Association.
