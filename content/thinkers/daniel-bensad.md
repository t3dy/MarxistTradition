Daniel Bensaid was a French Marxist philosopher and political activist who was a leading figure in the Trotskyist Fourth International and a founder of the Ligue Communiste Revolutionnaire. Radicalized during the May 1968 events in France, he remained committed to revolutionary politics throughout his life while producing a substantial body of philosophical work that sought to renew Marxist thought for contemporary conditions.

In *Marx for Our Times* (2002), Bensaid reinterpreted Marx through the lens of non-linear temporality and strategic reason, drawing on Walter Benjamin's conception of history to challenge both positivist and teleological readings of Marx. He argued that Marx's thought is structured around the logic of possibility and the strategic moment rather than historical inevitability. Bensaid's work represents a sophisticated attempt to maintain the revolutionary core of Marxism while engaging seriously with postmodern critiques of grand narratives and historical determinism.
