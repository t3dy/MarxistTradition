Christopher Hill was a British Marxist historian who transformed the study of the English Revolution of the seventeenth century. A member of the Communist Party Historians' Group alongside E.P. Thompson and Eric Hobsbawm, he served as Master of Balliol College, Oxford, and produced a vast body of scholarship that revealed the class conflicts and radical movements underlying the English Civil War.

In *The World Turned Upside Down* (1972), Hill recovered the voices of the Diggers, Levellers, Ranters, and other radical groups who challenged not only the monarchy but property relations and social hierarchy themselves. His work demonstrated that the English Revolution was not merely a constitutional conflict but a bourgeois revolution that released, and then suppressed, genuinely revolutionary popular energies. Hill's meticulous archival scholarship and his ability to connect intellectual and religious history to material social conditions exemplified the tradition of Marxist history from below and influenced generations of social historians.
