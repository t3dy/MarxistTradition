Michael Lowy is a Brazilian-French sociologist and philosopher who has made wide-ranging contributions to Marxist theory, from studies of the young Marx's revolutionary thought to analyses of romanticism, religion, and ecosocialism. A research director at the French National Centre for Scientific Research, he has worked to recover neglected dimensions of the Marxist tradition, particularly the romantic critique of capitalism and the intersection of Marxism with liberation theology.

In *The Theory of Revolution in the Young Marx*, Lowy traced the formation of Marx's revolutionary perspective, while *Fire Alarm* offered an influential reading of Walter Benjamin's theses on history as a critique of progressive, linear conceptions of historical development. Lowy has been a leading proponent of ecosocialism, arguing that ecological crisis demands a fundamental rethinking of Marxist categories of production and progress. His work bridges Latin American, European, and critical theory traditions, emphasizing the elective affinities between revolutionary romanticism and Marxist critique.
