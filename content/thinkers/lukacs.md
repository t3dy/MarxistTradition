Georg Lukács was a Hungarian philosopher and literary critic whose work helped inaugurate the tradition of Western Marxism. His concept of reification, developed in History and Class Consciousness, described how capitalist social relations come to appear as relations between things, obscuring the human labor underlying commodity exchange. He insisted that Marxism's distinguishing feature was not any particular thesis but its commitment to the category of totality as the foundation of revolutionary knowledge.
