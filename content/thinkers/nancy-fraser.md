Nancy Fraser is an American critical theorist whose work has reshaped the relationship between Marxist political economy and feminist theory. Her concept of capitalism as a 'cannibalistic' system that devours its own conditions of possibility — nature, social reproduction, public goods, political capacities — extends Marx's analysis beyond the point of production to encompass the full range of social relations that capital depends on but does not pay for. Cannibal Capitalism argues that the contemporary crisis is not merely economic but a general crisis in which capitalism's systematic devaluation of care work, ecological systems, and democratic governance undermines the very foundations on which accumulation depends.
