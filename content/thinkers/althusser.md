Louis Althusser proposed a rigorous rereading of Marx that distinguished between an early humanist period and a mature scientific one, arguing for an "epistemological break" in Marx's thought around 1845. His concept of overdetermination rejected simple base-superstructure causality in favor of a model in which multiple contradictions within a social formation interact and condense. His theory of ideology as constituting subjects through interpellation, developed in his essay on ideological state apparatuses, became one of the most widely debated frameworks in twentieth-century social theory.
