Rosa Luxemburg advanced an influential theory of capitalist accumulation that argued capitalism structurally depends on the incorporation of non-capitalist markets and territories for the realization of surplus value. Her political writings mounted a sustained critique of reformism within the social-democratic movement and theorized the mass strike as a form of revolutionary working-class self-activity. She was murdered by Freikorps paramilitaries in Berlin in January 1919.
