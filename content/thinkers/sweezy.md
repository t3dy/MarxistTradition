Paul Sweezy was an American Marxist economist and co-founder of Monthly Review, one of the most enduring independent socialist publications in the United States. His early Theory of Capitalist Development provided the first systematic English-language treatment of Marxist economics, while Monopoly Capital, co-authored with Paul Baran, argued that the concentration of corporate power had produced a tendency toward economic stagnation counteracted only by military spending, advertising, and financial speculation. His work established a distinctive school of Marxist political economy focused on monopoly, imperialism, and the structural tendencies of mature capitalism.
