Fredric Jameson was an American literary critic and political theorist who became the foremost Marxist cultural critic in the Anglophone world. His landmark analysis of postmodernism interpreted it not as a mere aesthetic style but as the cultural logic corresponding to a new stage of multinational capitalism characterized by the commodification of all spheres of life. In The Political Unconscious, he advanced the maxim that all cultural texts must ultimately be read as symbolic resolutions of real political and social contradictions.
