Karl Grün was a German journalist and political writer who became a leading figure of 'True Socialism,' a tendency Marx and Engels polemicised against extensively in *The German Ideology*. His major work, *The Social Movement in France and Belgium* (1845), attempted to present French socialist ideas — particularly those of Proudhon and Fourier — to a German audience.

Marx criticised Grün for translating concrete French social analysis into abstract German philosophical categories, stripping socialism of its material and class content. In *The Poverty of Philosophy*, Marx also attacked Grün's influence on Proudhon, whom Grün had introduced to Hegelian philosophy. The critique of True Socialism was essential to Marx and Engels's development of historical materialism: by distinguishing their scientific socialism from the philosophical idealism of Grün and others, they established that socialism must be grounded in the analysis of actual class relations and material conditions rather than abstract humanist appeals.
