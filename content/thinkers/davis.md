Angela Davis is an American political activist, scholar, and philosopher whose work has been central to connecting Marxist analysis with struggles against racial and gender oppression. Her Women, Race & Class provided a groundbreaking historical account of the interrelation of these three axes of domination in American society, while Are Prisons Obsolete? became a foundational text in the prison abolition movement. Throughout her career, she has insisted that meaningful freedom requires the simultaneous dismantling of capitalist exploitation, white supremacy, and patriarchy.
