Feargus O’Connor was the most prominent leader of the Chartist movement, the first mass working-class political movement in history. An Irish landowner and radical MP, O’Connor used his newspaper the *Northern Star* to build Chartism into a national movement demanding universal male suffrage, secret ballots, and annual parliaments.

O’Connor represented the physical force wing of Chartism, advocating mass mobilisation and the threat of insurrection over the moral suasion approach of William Lovett. He organised the great Chartist petition campaigns and the 1848 Kennington Common demonstration. Marx and Engels engaged critically with O’Connor, recognising Chartism’s significance as an independent working-class movement while criticising O’Connor’s land scheme as utopian and his leadership as demagogic. His mental collapse in 1852 symbolised the decline of Chartism as an organised force.
