Mao Zedong was the principal leader of the Chinese Communist Revolution and the founding chairman of the People's Republic of China. His theoretical contributions adapted Marxism-Leninism to the conditions of a predominantly agrarian society, placing the peasantry rather than the urban proletariat at the center of revolutionary strategy. His philosophical essays On Contradiction and On Practice developed a distinctive approach to materialist dialectics that emphasized the specificity of contradictions in any given situation and the primacy of practice as the criterion of truth.
