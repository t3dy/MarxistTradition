Cornelius Castoriadis was a Greek-French philosopher and political thinker who co-founded the influential journal *Socialisme ou Barbarie* (1948-1967). Originally a Trotskyist, he broke with orthodox Marxism to develop an autonomous theoretical framework centered on the creative capacity of human societies to institute themselves through what he called the social imaginary. His experiences analyzing the Soviet bureaucracy led him to argue that Marxism itself had become an ideology incapable of grasping the novelty of modern forms of domination.

In *The Imaginary Institution of Society* (1975), Castoriadis argued that societies are held together not by economic structures or rational calculation but by collectively created systems of meaning. He maintained that genuine autonomy requires the conscious recognition that social institutions are human creations and can be deliberately transformed. While he eventually abandoned Marxism, his critique of bureaucratic capitalism and his insistence on workers' self-management profoundly influenced the autonomist tradition and post-Marxist thought.
