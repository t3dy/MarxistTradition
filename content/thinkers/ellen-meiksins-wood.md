Ellen Meiksins Wood was an American-Canadian political theorist and historian who was a leading figure in the tradition of Political Marxism. An editor of *Monthly Review* and professor at York University, she developed a distinctive approach to Marxist theory that emphasized the historically specific and contingent origins of capitalism against both liberal and Marxist accounts that treated capitalism as a natural outcome of human commercial instincts.

In *The Origin of Capitalism* (1999), Wood argued that capitalism originated not from the expansion of trade but from the transformation of English agrarian property relations that compelled both landlords and tenants to compete on the market, making market dependence a condition of survival. *Democracy Against Capitalism* (1995) contended that liberal democracy, far from being antagonistic to capitalism, serves to separate the political from the economic, insulating capitalist exploitation from democratic accountability. Her work challenged teleological assumptions in Marxist historiography and insisted on the radical specificity of capitalism as a historically novel social form.
