Ernest Mandel was a Belgian economist and political activist who became the most prominent Marxist economic theorist within the Trotskyist movement. His Late Capitalism offered a comprehensive analysis of postwar capitalist development, periodizing it as a third stage of capitalism characterized by the generalization of mechanization to all sectors of the economy. His work on long waves of capitalist development sought to integrate technological innovation and class struggle into an explanation of the recurring cycles of expansion and crisis that define capitalist history.
