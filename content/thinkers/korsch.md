Karl Korsch was a German Marxist theorist and politician who argued that Marxism must be understood as a living philosophy rather than a fixed doctrine. His 1923 work Marxism and Philosophy insisted that the relationship between theory and practice was itself a dialectical problem, challenging both Social Democratic revisionism and the mechanical materialism of orthodox party Marxism. Expelled from the Communist Party for his heterodox views, he became an important precursor to council communism and left-communist currents.
