Etienne Cabet was a French political theorist and utopian socialist who advocated for a peaceful transition to communism through the establishment of model communities. Originally a liberal republican, he radicalized during exile in England where he encountered Owen's ideas. His novel *Voyage to Icaria* (1840) described an ideal communist society governed by democratic principles, with common ownership of property and production organized for collective benefit.

Cabet attracted a significant following among French workers and artisans, and in 1848 led several hundred followers to establish an Icarian colony in Texas, later relocated to Illinois. The colonies struggled with internal conflicts and material hardships. Marx and Engels regarded Cabet as representative of a naive, pre-scientific socialism that sought to realize communism through moral example rather than through understanding the contradictions of capitalism. Nevertheless, Cabet's popular communism helped spread egalitarian ideas among the French working class during a formative period.
