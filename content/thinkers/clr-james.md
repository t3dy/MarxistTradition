C.L.R. James was a Trinidadian-born historian, journalist, and revolutionary theorist whose work bridged Caribbean anti-colonialism, Pan-Africanism, and heterodox Marxism. The Black Jacobins, his masterwork, recovered the Haitian Revolution as a world-historical event driven by the self-emancipation of enslaved people, challenging Eurocentric narratives of revolutionary agency. His wide-ranging intellectual life encompassed Hegelian dialectics, cricket as cultural expression, and an unwavering commitment to the creative capacity of ordinary people to make their own history.
