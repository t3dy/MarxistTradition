Istvan Meszaros was a Hungarian-British Marxist philosopher who studied under Georg Lukacs in Budapest before emigrating to Britain after the 1956 Hungarian uprising. His early work on Marx's theory of alienation established him as a leading interpreter of the humanist dimensions of Marx's thought. He spent decades developing a comprehensive critique of what he termed the "capital system" as a mode of social metabolic control.

In his monumental work *Beyond Capital* (1995), Meszaros argued that the capital system is broader than capitalism and that Soviet-type societies represented a form of post-capitalist capital rather than socialism. He maintained that partial reforms are structurally incapable of overcoming capital's logic and that a radical social metabolic transformation is necessary. His insistence on the systemic character of capital's domination and his critique of reformist and parliamentary strategies have made his work influential among Marxists seeking to theorize the totality of capitalist social relations and the requirements for genuine socialist transition.
