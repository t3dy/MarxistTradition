Perry Anderson is a British Marxist historian and essayist who served as editor of *New Left Review* for several decades, shaping it into one of the most important journals of Marxist intellectual life. His work spans historical sociology, political theory, and the history of ideas, combining analytical rigor with a sweeping command of European history and social theory.

In *Lineages of the Absolutist State* (1974) and its companion volume *Passages from Antiquity to Feudalism*, Anderson offered a systematic Marxist account of state formation in Europe and the transition from feudalism to capitalism. *Considerations on Western Marxism* (1976) provided an influential mapping of the shift in Marxist theory from political economy and strategy toward philosophy and aesthetics after the defeats of the interwar period. Anderson's work is notable for its insistence on combining structural analysis with attention to geopolitical and conjunctural specificity, and for its sustained engagement with questions of state power, class formation, and historical transition.
