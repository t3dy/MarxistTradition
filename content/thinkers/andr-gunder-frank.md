Andre Gunder Frank was a German-American economic historian and sociologist who became one of the most influential theorists of dependency and underdevelopment. His work challenged modernization theory by arguing that the poverty of the Third World was not a result of backwardness but was actively produced by its integration into the capitalist world system.

In *Capitalism and Underdevelopment in Latin America* (1967), Frank argued that a chain of metropolis-satellite relations transferred surplus from the periphery to the center, creating what he termed "the development of underdevelopment." This thesis generated enormous debate within Marxist and development studies. Critics argued that Frank conflated exchange relations with capitalist production relations and paid insufficient attention to internal class structures. In his later work *ReOrient* (1998), Frank radically revised his framework, arguing that Asia had been the center of the world economy for most of human history and that European dominance was a recent and potentially temporary phenomenon.
