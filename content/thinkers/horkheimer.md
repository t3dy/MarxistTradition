Max Horkheimer was the director of the Frankfurt Institute for Social Research and a founding figure of critical theory. His programmatic essay "Traditional and Critical Theory" distinguished between positivist science, which accepts the given social order, and critical theory, which aims at human emancipation by revealing the historical conditions of knowledge. Together with Adorno, he analyzed how instrumental reason had become the dominant mode of thought in modern capitalist societies, subordinating all values to calculability and efficiency.
