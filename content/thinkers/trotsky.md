Leon Trotsky was a leading figure in the Russian Revolution of 1917 and the principal organizer of the Red Army during the Russian Civil War. He developed the theory of permanent revolution, arguing that socialist transformation in underdeveloped countries could not halt at the bourgeois-democratic stage but must proceed uninterruptedly toward socialism. After losing the power struggle with Stalin, he was exiled and later assassinated in Mexico, but his writings on bureaucratic degeneration and internationalism remained influential across the global left.
