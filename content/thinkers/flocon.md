Ferdinand Flocon was a French republican journalist and politician who edited the radical newspaper *La Réforme* and served in the Provisional Government following the February Revolution of 1848. He represented the democratic republican left, positioned between the moderate republicanism of Lamartine and the socialism of Louis Blanc.

Flocon maintained connections with Marx and the Communist League, and it was Flocon who, as a member of the Provisional Government, formally invited Marx to return to France after the revolution. His newspaper *La Réforme* had provided a platform for democratic and socialist ideas in the years leading up to 1848. Marx's analysis of the 1848 revolution situated figures like Flocon within the democratic petty bourgeoisie — a class fraction capable of radical rhetoric but ultimately unable to break with bourgeois property relations or support independent working-class politics.
