Anne Robert Jacques Turgot was a French economist and statesman who served as Controller-General of Finances under Louis XVI. A leading figure of the Physiocratic school, he attempted to implement liberal economic reforms including the abolition of guild restrictions and the corvee (forced labor). His reform program was blocked by aristocratic opposition, and he was dismissed in 1776, foreshadowing the revolutionary crisis that would follow.

His *Reflections on the Formation and Distribution of Wealth* (1766) advanced a sophisticated analysis of capital accumulation, the division of labor, and the role of the agricultural surplus in economic development. Turgot distinguished between different classes based on their relationship to production and explored how wealth concentrates through the dynamics of investment and return. Marx regarded Turgot as one of the more perceptive Physiocrats, noting his contributions to understanding the relationship between labor, land, and surplus, even as Marx criticized the Physiocratic limitation of productive labor to agriculture alone.
