Michael Hardt is an American political philosopher and literary theorist who, in collaboration with Antonio Negri, has produced some of the most widely discussed works of contemporary Marxist theory. A professor at Duke University, Hardt brings together the Italian autonomist tradition with post-structuralist philosophy and an analysis of globalization to theorize new forms of sovereignty and resistance.

The *Empire* trilogy, co-authored with Negri, argues that the classical imperialism of nation-states has been superseded by a decentered, networked form of global sovereignty that Hardt and Negri call Empire. Against this stands the multitude, a new revolutionary subject composed of diverse singularities whose immaterial and affective labor creates the common wealth that Empire seeks to expropriate. Their work has been both celebrated for its ambitious rethinking of power and resistance in the era of globalization and criticized for underestimating the continued importance of nation-states and traditional class formations.
