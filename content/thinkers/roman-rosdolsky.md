Roman Rosdolsky was a Ukrainian Marxist scholar and political activist whose study of Marx's *Grundrisse* became a foundational text for the renaissance of Marxist economic theory. Active in the revolutionary movement in interwar Galicia, he survived the Auschwitz concentration camp and emigrated to the United States, where he spent his remaining years in scholarly work.

His major work, *The Making of Marx's Capital* (1968), was among the first comprehensive studies of the *Grundrisse*, which had only recently been published in full. Rosdolsky traced the development of Marx's plan for *Capital* from the original six-book outline through its various transformations, illuminating the methodological foundations of Marx's critique of political economy. His careful reconstruction of Marx's categories of value, money, and capital demonstrated the centrality of the Hegelian dialectic to Marx's mature economic thought and helped establish the *Grundrisse* as an essential text for understanding Marx's intellectual project.
