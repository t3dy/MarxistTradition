Erik Olin Wright was an American sociologist who was the leading figure in Analytical Marxism's approach to class analysis. A professor at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, he devoted his career to developing a rigorous, empirically testable Marxist theory of class structure in advanced capitalist societies.

In *Classes* (1985), Wright addressed the problem of "contradictory class locations," positions in the class structure that do not fit neatly into the bourgeoisie-proletariat binary, such as managers and professionals who possess organizational or credential assets while remaining employees. He developed a multi-dimensional framework that could account for the complexity of modern class structures while retaining exploitation as the core concept. In his later work *Envisioning Real Utopias* (2010), Wright shifted toward identifying actually existing institutional alternatives to capitalism, from worker cooperatives to participatory budgeting, arguing that Marxists must combine critique with constructive proposals for emancipatory social transformation.
