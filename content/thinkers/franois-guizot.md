François Guizot was a French liberal historian and statesman who served as Prime Minister under Louis-Philippe's July Monarchy (1840-1848). His historical works, particularly *History of Civilization in Europe*, developed a theory of class struggle as the motor of European history — an insight Marx acknowledged while criticising Guizot's bourgeois limitations.

Guizot's historiography recognised that modern European history had been shaped by conflicts between nobility, bourgeoisie, and common people, making him part of the French historical school (alongside Thierry and Mignet) that Marx credited with discovering the class struggle. However, Guizot treated the triumph of the bourgeoisie as history's endpoint, naturalising capitalist class relations. His government's defence of the grande bourgeoisie's monopoly on political power — epitomised by his response to demands for suffrage reform: 'Enrichissez-vous' — provoked the February Revolution that overthrew the July Monarchy in 1848.
