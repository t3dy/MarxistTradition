Franco 'Bifo' Berardi is an Italian autonomist theorist whose work analyses the psychopathology of contemporary capitalism. His concept of 'semio-capitalism' — capitalism in which the primary source of accumulation is the production and exchange of signs and information — connects the autonomist analysis of cognitive labour to an account of widespread depression, anxiety, and burnout as structural effects of capital's colonisation of mental life. The Soul at Work argues that when capital puts the soul to work — requiring creativity, affect, and communication as productive inputs — the result is not liberation but a new and more intimate form of exploitation that leaves no dimension of human experience outside the valorisation process.
