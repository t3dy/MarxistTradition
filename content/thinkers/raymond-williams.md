Raymond Williams was a Welsh cultural theorist, novelist, and critic who was one of the founding figures of cultural studies and a leading representative of the British New Left. Born into a working-class family in a Welsh border village, he studied at Cambridge and became a prolific writer whose work transformed the study of culture within the Marxist tradition.

In *Culture and Society* (1958) and *The Long Revolution* (1961), Williams traced the development of the idea of culture in English thought, arguing that culture is not a separate aesthetic realm but an integral dimension of social life. His concept of "cultural materialism" sought to analyze cultural production as a material social process, overcoming the base-superstructure model that reduced culture to a mere reflection of economic relations. In *Marxism and Literature* (1977), he developed concepts such as "structures of feeling" and the distinction between dominant, residual, and emergent cultural formations that provided tools for analyzing the lived experience of social change.
