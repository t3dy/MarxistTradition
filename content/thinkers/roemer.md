John Roemer reformulated Marxian exploitation theory using the tools of neoclassical economics, game theory, and general equilibrium analysis, arguing that exploitation can be rigorously defined in terms of unequal distribution of productive assets rather than the labor theory of value. As a founding figure of Analytical Marxism, he sought to reconstruct core Marxist claims using methodological individualism and formal modeling. His work shifted debate on exploitation toward questions of distributive justice and property relations, influencing both Marxist economic theory and liberal political philosophy.
