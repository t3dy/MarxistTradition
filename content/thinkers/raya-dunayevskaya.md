Raya Dunayevskaya was a Ukrainian-American Marxist philosopher who founded the tradition of Marxist-Humanism in the United States. She served briefly as Leon Trotsky's secretary before breaking with Trotskyism to develop her theory that the Soviet Union represented a form of state capitalism rather than a deformed workers' state. She co-led the Johnson-Forest Tendency with C.L.R. James before establishing her own organization, News and Letters Committees.

In *Marxism and Freedom* (1958) and *Philosophy and Revolution* (1973), Dunayevskaya argued that Marx's philosophical humanism, rooted in Hegel's dialectic, was inseparable from his economic analysis. She insisted that the movement from practice, particularly the self-activity of workers, women, and colonized peoples, constitutes a form of theory. Her recovery of Marx's 1844 Manuscripts and her emphasis on the Hegelian foundations of Marx's thought contributed to a broader reassessment of the relationship between Marxist philosophy and revolutionary practice.
