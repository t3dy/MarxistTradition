Theodor W. Adorno was a German philosopher, sociologist, and musicologist who became one of the most prominent members of the Frankfurt School. With Max Horkheimer, he authored Dialectic of Enlightenment, which traced how the Enlightenment's drive to dominate nature had paradoxically produced new forms of domination and barbarism. His later Negative Dialectics sought to sustain critical thought without resolving contradictions into a totalizing synthesis, insisting that philosophy must attend to the non-identical and the particular.
