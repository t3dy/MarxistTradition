Alphonse de Lamartine was a French romantic poet and politician who played a leading role in the February Revolution of 1848, serving as head of the Provisional Government of the Second Republic. His *History of the Girondins* popularised a sympathetic account of the French Revolution that helped inspire the 1848 uprising.

Lamartine represented the moderate republican bourgeoisie that initially led the revolution but proved unable to satisfy working-class demands. Marx analysed Lamartine's role in *The Class Struggles in France*, showing how the provisional government used republican rhetoric to contain proletarian aspirations while preserving bourgeois property relations. Lamartine's rapid displacement by more conservative forces — and the subsequent June Days massacre of Parisian workers — demonstrated the class limits of bourgeois republicanism that Marx would theorise as fundamental to understanding the modern state.
