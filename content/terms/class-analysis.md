Class analysis (Klassenanalyse) designates the theoretical and empirical investigation of class structure, class positions, and class struggle within a given social formation. Marx's own class analysis, developed across works from *The Eighteenth Brumaire* to *Capital*, combined structural determination by relations of production with attention to political conjuncture, ideology, and fractional divisions within classes. Subsequent Marxist debates have centered on the boundary problem â€” where to draw the line between proletariat and bourgeoisie, and how to theorize intermediate strata. Poulantzas emphasized structural class places defined by economic, political, and ideological criteria, while E.O. Wright developed models of contradictory class locations. Autonomist and feminist Marxists have challenged productivist frameworks by insisting on the class significance of unwaged labor, social reproduction, and racialized divisions. Class analysis remains indispensable for understanding power, exploitation, and political possibility under capitalism.
