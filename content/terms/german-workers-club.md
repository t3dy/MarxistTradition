The German Workers' Club (Deutscher Arbeiterverein) was founded in Paris in 1848 by German émigré workers and intellectuals, with Marx serving as president. The club aimed to organise German workers in France and coordinate with the broader revolutionary movement during the 1848 upheavals.

Marx used the organisation to promote the positions of the Communist League and to counter rival tendencies, including utopian socialists and democratic nationalists. The German Workers' Club represents an early instance of Marx's direct engagement in practical political organisation alongside his theoretical work.
