Exchange relations designate the social relationships constituted through the buying and selling of commodities, including the exchange of labour-power for wages. Marx distinguished exchange relations from production relations while insisting on their internal connection: commodities enter exchange as products of alienated labour, and exchange both presupposes and reproduces class relations.

In *Capital*, Marx demonstrates that the apparent equality and freedom of exchange conceals the extraction of surplus value in production. Exchange relations generate commodity fetishism through which social relations between producers appear as relations between things. Debates persist over whether exchange relations are merely derivative of production or possess their own constitutive logic.
