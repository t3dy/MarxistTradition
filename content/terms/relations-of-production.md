Relations of production are the social relations people enter into in the process of producing their material life, encompassing property relations, the division of labor, and the distribution of the social product. Under capitalism, the fundamental relation of production is between those who own the means of production and those who must sell their labor-power. Marx argues that at a certain stage of development, the existing relations of production become fetters upon the productive forces, opening an epoch of social revolution.
