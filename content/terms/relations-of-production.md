Relations of production (*Produktionsverhaltnisse*) designate the social relations into which human beings enter in the process of producing their material life — principally the relations of ownership and control over the means of production, the form of appropriation of surplus labor, and the division of labor within and between enterprises. Under capitalism, the fundamental relation of production is the wage-labor/capital relation: the class of propertyless workers sells labor-power to the class that monopolizes the means of production, and surplus-value is extracted through the difference between the value labor-power creates and its cost of reproduction. Relations of production are distinguished from forces of production (the technical means of production), and the contradiction between the two — as developing productive forces strain against existing social relations — constitutes the material foundation of social revolution in Marx's theory of history. The concept is broader than mere property law, encompassing the real social relationships through which production is organized, surplus is distributed, and classes are constituted.
