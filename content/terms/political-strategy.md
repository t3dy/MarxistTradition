Political strategy in Marxist theory encompasses the diverse approaches to achieving revolutionary transformation of capitalist society. The mass party strategy, developed by the Second International, sought to build large workers' parties combining electoral activity with union organisation. The united front strategy, adopted by the early Comintern, proposed alliances between revolutionary and reformist workers' organisations for common goals while maintaining political independence.

The popular front strategy broadened alliances to include liberal bourgeois forces against fascism, generating controversy over class collaboration. Dual power strategy, theorised from the Russian soviets, envisages parallel institutions of workers' power challenging bourgeois state authority. Insurrectionary strategy centres on the armed seizure of state power at moments of crisis. Mao's mass line and protracted people's war adapted strategy to peasant-based revolution. Democratic centralism governs internal party organisation. Contemporary debates address whether traditional strategies remain viable under neoliberal conditions or whether new forms of political organisation are required.
