The bourgeoisie is the class that owns the means of production and appropriates surplus value through the exploitation of wage labor. In *The Communist Manifesto*, Marx and Engels portrayed the bourgeoisie as a historically revolutionary class that dissolved feudal relations, created the world market, and revolutionized the forces of production, while simultaneously generating its own gravedigger in the proletariat. Marx distinguished between the industrial bourgeoisie, the commercial bourgeoisie, and the financial bourgeoisie, each with distinct interests that could produce intra-class conflict. The concept has been refined through debates on the petty bourgeoisie, the managerial stratum, and the relationship between bourgeois class power and state form. Poulantzas analyzed the bourgeoisie as a fractured class unified politically through the capitalist state, while world-systems theorists emphasize the transnational character of bourgeois class formation under imperialism.
