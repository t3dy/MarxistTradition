In Marxist theory, the constitution is analyzed not as a neutral legal framework guaranteeing universal rights but as a juridical form that codifies and secures bourgeois class rule. Marx's critique in *On the Jewish Question* and *The Eighteenth Brumaire* reveals how constitutional rights presuppose and reproduce the separation between the political state and civil society, granting formal equality to citizens while leaving substantive inequality in property relations untouched. The constitutional guarantee of property rights is foundational to the legal architecture of capitalist exploitation. Gramsci analyzed constitutions as elements of hegemonic order, functioning within both political and civil society.
