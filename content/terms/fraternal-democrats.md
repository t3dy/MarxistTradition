The Fraternal Democrats was an internationalist organisation founded in London in 1845, bringing together radical émigrés and British Chartists. Members included Julian Harney, Ernest Jones, and various European revolutionaries connected to Marx and Engels. The organisation represented an early attempt at international working-class coordination, anticipating the International Working Men's Association founded two decades later.

The Fraternal Democrats promoted solidarity among democratic and proletarian movements across national boundaries. Marx and Engels maintained contact with the group, and its internationalist orientation influenced the development of communist political organisation, reflecting the growing recognition that national liberation struggles and class emancipation were interconnected.
