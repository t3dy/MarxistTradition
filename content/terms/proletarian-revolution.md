Proletarian revolution designates the process by which the working class overthrows capitalist social relations and establishes its own political rule as a transition to classless society. Marx theorized this as the necessary outcome of intensifying contradictions between the forces and relations of production, distinguishing it from all previous revolutions by its aim to abolish class society altogether rather than replace one ruling class with another. Lenin developed the theory further in *State and Revolution*, arguing that the bourgeois state must be smashed rather than gradually reformed. Central debates concern the conditions for revolution, the role of the vanguard party, the relationship between democratic and socialist tasks, and whether revolution requires catastrophic crisis or can emerge from prolonged structural transformation.
