Industrial capital is the form of capital engaged directly in the production of commodities through the employment of wage labour. Marx distinguished it from merchant capital, which operates only in the sphere of circulation, and interest-bearing capital, which lends money for a return. In *Capital* Volume 2, Marx analysed industrial capital's circuit through three forms: money capital, productive capital, and commodity capital.

Industrial capital's dominance over merchant and usury capital marked the specifically capitalist mode of production, subordinating all social relations to the extraction of surplus value in production. The historical rise of industrial capital through the Industrial Revolution transformed artisanal production into factory-based manufacture and large-scale industry, creating the modern proletariat as a class.
