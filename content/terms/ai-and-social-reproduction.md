AI and social reproduction examines how artificial intelligence transforms the processes through which labor power is produced, maintained, and reproduced — education, healthcare, domestic labor, childcare, and the broader infrastructure of care that sustains the working population. Feminist Marxists have long argued that capitalism depends on a vast domain of unwaged reproductive labor — predominantly performed by women — that produces the labor power capital exploits. AI intervenes in this domain through automated education platforms, diagnostic algorithms, elder-care robots, and domestic AI assistants, raising the question of whether these technologies liberate reproductive labor or further devalue it.

The deployment of AI in education automates the reproduction of ideologically compliant labor power, while AI in healthcare optimises the maintenance of workers according to productivity metrics rather than human need. More fundamentally, AI transforms the reproduction of ideology — the process through which subjects are formed who accept existing social relations as natural and inevitable. When algorithmic systems mediate the information environment through which people understand the world, the reproduction of ideological consent is automated and intensified beyond anything the traditional ideological apparatuses of school, church, and media could achieve. The struggle over AI in social reproduction is ultimately a struggle over the conditions under which human beings are produced — whether they are formed as autonomous subjects capable of critical thought and collective action, or as optimised inputs for a production process that extends from the factory to the totality of social life.
