Trotskyism debates refer to the theoretical and political controversies surrounding the legacy of Leon Trotsky and the movement bearing his name. Central to Trotskyism are the theory of permanent revolution, which rejects stageist models of socialist development; the critique of Stalinist bureaucratic degeneration, formulated in *The Revolution Betrayed*; and the transitional program as a bridge between immediate demands and revolutionary objectives. The Fourth International, founded by Trotsky in 1938, subsequently fragmented into numerous competing organizations, each claiming authentic continuity with Trotsky's legacy. Key debates concern whether the Soviet Union was a degenerated workers' state, state capitalist, or a new form of class society; the validity of the transitional method; the applicability of permanent revolution to postcolonial contexts; and whether Trotskyism's organizational sectarianism has undermined its political effectiveness despite the analytical power of its theoretical contributions.
