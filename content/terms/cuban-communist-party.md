The Cuban Communist Party (Partido Comunista de Cuba, PCC), formally established in 1965 through the merger of revolutionary organizations led by Fidel Castro, has been the sole ruling party of Cuba since the revolution. Its formation followed the consolidation of the 26th of July Movement, the Popular Socialist Party, and the Revolutionary Directorate. The PCC's trajectory raises important questions for Marxist theory concerning the relationship between guerrilla movements and proletarian organization, the viability of socialist construction in a small peripheral economy under imperial blockade, and the dynamics of single-party rule.
