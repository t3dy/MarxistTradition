Gender and labour analysis examines the gendered division of productive and reproductive work within capitalism. Marxist feminists have argued that women's unpaid domestic labour — childcare, housework, emotional sustenance — is essential to reproducing labour-power but rendered invisible by capital's focus on waged production.

The domestic labour debate of the 1970s, involving Dalla Costa, James, and Seccombe, asked whether housework produced value. Social reproduction theory, developed by Bhattacharya and others, extends this analysis to encompass the totality of activities required to reproduce labour-power. Debates continue over whether gender oppression is reducible to capitalist relations or constitutes a relatively autonomous system intersecting with class.
