Feudal socialism refers to a reactionary critique of capitalism mounted from the standpoint of the displaced feudal aristocracy. Marx and Engels address it in the *Communist Manifesto* as one of several varieties of non-proletarian socialism. Feudal socialists denounced bourgeois society for destroying traditional social bonds and organic community, but their critique served aristocratic class interests rather than emancipatory ones.

Marx noted the half-satirical, half-reactionary character of this tendency, which wielded the proletariat's grievances as a weapon against the bourgeoisie without genuine concern for working-class liberation. This category illuminates Marx's method of distinguishing between critiques of capitalism based on their class standpoint and historical direction.
