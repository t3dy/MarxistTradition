The monetary system encompasses the institutions, mechanisms, and social relations through which money functions in capitalist economies. Marx analysed money as arising from the commodity form itself: as the universal equivalent, money is the necessary expression of value and the medium through which abstract labour achieves social validity. In *Capital* Volume 1, he traced money's functions as measure of value, means of circulation, means of payment, and world money.

Marx's analysis of banking and credit in *Capital* Volume 3 examined how the credit system simultaneously socialises capital and intensifies its contradictions. Hilferding's *Finance Capital* analysed the fusion of banking and industrial capital. Contemporary debates address fiat money, central banking, financialisation, cryptocurrency, and Modern Monetary Theory, all of which raise questions about the relationship between money, value, and state power that remain contested within Marxist theory.
