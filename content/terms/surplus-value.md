---
term: Surplus-Value
german: Mehrwert
source: "Capital Vol. 1, Ch. 7"
related: [exploitation, labor-power, variable-capital]
tags: [value theory, exploitation, political economy]
---

Surplus-value is the value produced by the worker in excess of the value of their labor-power, appropriated without equivalent by the capitalist. It arises because the use-value of labor-power -- its capacity to create value -- exceeds its own exchange-value as determined by the cost of the worker's reproduction. Surplus-value is the specific form that exploitation takes under capitalism and constitutes the basis of profit, interest, and rent.
