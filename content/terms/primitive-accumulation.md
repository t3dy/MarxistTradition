Primitive accumulation designates the historical process by which producers were separated from the means of production, creating the preconditions for capitalist relations. It encompasses the enclosure of common lands, colonial plunder, the slave trade, and the use of state violence to create a class of propertyless workers compelled to sell their labor-power. Marx's account demonstrates that the origin of capital is not peaceful saving or industry but force and dispossession.
