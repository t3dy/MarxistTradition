---
term: Neoliberal Globalization
tags: [theory, political economy]
traditions:
  classical: "Neoliberal globalisation is the latest phase of imperialism, using international institutions and free trade agreements to enforce the interests of metropolitan capital, dismantle workers' protections, and deepen the exploitation of the Global South."
  value-form: "Neoliberal globalisation represents the value-form's deepened penetration of all social relations worldwide, as structural adjustment and trade liberalisation enforce the discipline of abstract labour through market competition on a planetary scale."
---

Neoliberal globalisation refers to the restructuring of the world economy from the late 1970s onward through trade liberalisation, financial deregulation, privatisation, and the imposition of market discipline via international institutions such as the IMF, World Bank, and WTO. This project responded to the profitability crisis of the 1970s by attacking organised labour, dismantling welfare states, and opening new territories for capital accumulation.

Structural adjustment programmes imposed austerity on indebted nations of the Global South, forcing privatisation and export-oriented production. Transnational corporations exploited global wage differentials through offshore production and export processing zones. Harvey theorised neoliberalism as a class project to restore elite power through accumulation by dispossession. Critics from various Marxist traditions debate whether neoliberalism constitutes a new stage of capitalism, a restoration of classical liberal imperialism, or a response to the structural contradictions of Fordist accumulation.
