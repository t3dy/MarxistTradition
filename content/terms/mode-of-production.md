---
term: Mode of Production
german: Produktionsweise

---

A mode of production is the historically specific unity of productive forces and relations of production that characterizes a given form of society. It encompasses not only the technical means by which goods are produced but also the social relations under which production takes place, including property relations and the division of labor. Marx identifies several successive modes of production -- including ancient, feudal, and capitalist -- each defined by a distinct form of surplus extraction and class antagonism.
