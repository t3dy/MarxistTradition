Class struggle is the antagonistic conflict between social classes arising from their opposed positions within the relations of production. Marx and Engels famously declared that the history of all hitherto existing society is the history of class struggles, positioning it as the primary motor of historical change and the mechanism through which one mode of production gives way to another. In capitalist society, the fundamental class struggle takes place between the bourgeoisie, who own the means of production, and the proletariat, who must sell their labor-power to survive. This struggle manifests in direct economic conflict over wages, working conditions, and the length of the working day, but also in political struggles over state power and in ideological struggles over the legitimacy of existing social arrangements. Marx argued that the intensification of class contradictions under capitalism would ultimately produce the conditions for proletarian revolution, though the forms, timing, and outcomes of class struggle are shaped by historically specific conjunctures that no general theory can predict in advance.
