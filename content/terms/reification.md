Reification describes the process by which social relations between people take on the appearance of relations between things, extending Marx's analysis of commodity fetishism into a comprehensive critique of bourgeois consciousness. Lukacs developed the concept to show how the commodity form penetrates all aspects of life under capitalism, producing a fragmented, contemplative stance toward a reality that appears as a collection of fixed, natural objects. The concept bridges the critique of political economy and the theory of consciousness, linking the structural logic of capital to the lived experience of its subjects.
