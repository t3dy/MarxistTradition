The February Revolution of 1848 in France overthrew the July Monarchy of Louis-Philippe and established the Second Republic. Triggered by economic crisis and demands for electoral reform, the revolution united workers, students, and sections of the bourgeoisie against aristocratic finance capital. The provisional government's National Workshops represented a concession to working-class demands, but the subsequent June Days revealed irreconcilable class antagonism.

Marx's analysis in *The Class Struggles in France* and *The Eighteenth Brumaire* used the 1848 experience to develop his theory of class alliances, bonapartism, and the state, arguing that the bourgeoisie ultimately preferred authoritarian rule to genuine democracy when its class interests were threatened.
