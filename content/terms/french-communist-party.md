The French Communist Party (PCF) was founded in 1920 at the Tours Congress when the majority of the Socialist Party voted to join the Communist International. The PCF became a mass party with deep roots in the industrial working class, playing a significant role in the Popular Front of 1936, the Resistance, and postwar French politics.

Under Thorez, the party adhered closely to Soviet direction. Its theoretical contributions included Althusser's structural Marxism and the party's engagement with Eurocommunism under Marchais in the 1970s. The PCF's decline from the 1980s onward reflected broader crises of official communism and the restructuring of the French working class.
