The Industrial Revolution, originating in late eighteenth-century Britain, transformed production from handicraft and manufacture to machine-based factory industry. Marx analysed this transition in *Capital* Volume 1 as the movement from formal to real subsumption of labour under capital, where machinery revolutionised the labour process itself rather than merely organising existing techniques under capitalist command.

Engels documented the social consequences in *The Condition of the Working Class in England*: mass urbanisation, the destruction of artisanal livelihoods, child labour, and the formation of an industrial proletariat. E.P. Thompson's *The Making of the English Working Class* traced how workers forged collective identity and political consciousness through resistance to industrialisation. The revolution depended on colonial wealth, enclosure of common lands, and the forcible creation of a propertyless workforce.
