Contemporary political economy refers to the ongoing Marxist analysis of capitalism's present phase, encompassing debates on financialization, neoliberalism, platform capitalism, global value chains, and the transformation of labor under digital technologies. Building on Marx's critique of political economy, contemporary practitioners investigate how the fundamental categories â€” value, surplus value, accumulation, crisis â€” manifest under current conditions. Key debates include whether financialization represents a structural shift in accumulation or a symptom of declining profitability in production, whether digital platforms create new forms of rent extraction or value production, and how global labor arbitrage reshapes class composition across the world system. Theorists such as David Harvey, Robert Brenner, Anwar Shaikh, and the New Reading of Marx school have contributed significantly to these analyses. The field also engages with ecological crisis, social reproduction theory, and the racialized dimensions of contemporary accumulation.
