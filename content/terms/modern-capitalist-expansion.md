Modern capitalist expansion refers to the historical process through which the capitalist mode of production extended from its European origins to encompass the entire globe. Marx and Engels described in the *Communist Manifesto* how the bourgeoisie creates a world after its own image, battering down all Chinese walls through the cheap prices of its commodities.

This expansion proceeded through distinct phases: mercantilist colonialism and the Atlantic slave trade (16th-18th centuries), free trade imperialism and the scramble for Africa (19th century), monopoly capitalism and inter-imperialist war (early 20th century), and neoliberal globalisation (late 20th century onward). Luxemburg argued that capitalism requires non-capitalist environments to absorb surplus production. Harvey's concept of accumulation by dispossession describes how contemporary expansion continues to enclose commons, privatise public goods, and open new territories for capital accumulation.
