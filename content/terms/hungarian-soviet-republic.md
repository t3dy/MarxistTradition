The Hungarian Soviet Republic, established in March 1919 under Béla Kun, lasted 133 days before being overthrown by Romanian military intervention and internal counter-revolution. It emerged when the Social Democrats merged with the Communists and proclaimed a Soviet government without armed insurrection.

The republic implemented nationalisations and workers' councils but faced economic crisis, military encirclement, and insufficient peasant support due to failure to distribute land. Lenin and the Comintern drew lessons about the inadequacy of merging with social democracy. The subsequent White Terror under Horthy decimated the Hungarian left for a generation.
