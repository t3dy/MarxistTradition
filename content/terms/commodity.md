A commodity is an external object that satisfies human wants and is produced for exchange rather than direct use. It possesses a twofold character: use-value, which addresses a particular need, and value, which expresses the abstract social labor objectified within it. Marx begins *Capital* with the commodity because it is the most elementary form of wealth in capitalist societies, and its analysis reveals the fundamental contradictions of the capitalist mode of production.
