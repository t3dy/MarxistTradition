Colonial capitalism refers to the specific form of capitalist development imposed through European colonial expansion, in which the extraction of surplus from colonized territories was integral to the accumulation process in the metropole. Marx addressed colonialism in his discussions of primitive accumulation in *Capital*, identifying conquest, plunder, enslavement, and the destruction of precapitalist economies as foundational to the emergence of industrial capitalism. Debates persist over whether colonialism was a necessary stage in capitalist development, whether it primarily served mercantilist or industrial capital, and how racial domination articulated with class exploitation. World-systems theory, postcolonial Marxism, and racial capitalism frameworks continue to develop this analysis.
