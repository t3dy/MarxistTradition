The value-form is the mode in which the value of a commodity is expressed through its relation to another commodity. Marx traces the development of the value-form from the simple or accidental form, through the expanded and general forms, to the money-form. This analysis demonstrates that money is not an arbitrary convention but the necessary result of the contradictions inherent in the commodity-form itself.
