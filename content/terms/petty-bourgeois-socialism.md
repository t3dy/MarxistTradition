Petty-bourgeois socialism is a tendency Marx and Engels criticised in the *Communist Manifesto* and elsewhere for opposing capitalism from the standpoint of the small producer rather than the proletariat. Sismondi was identified as its chief representative, denouncing the concentration of capital and the destruction of artisanal production while proposing a return to small-scale property relations.

This tendency reappears historically in movements that resist proletarianisation — defending small farms, shops, and workshops against corporate concentration — without recognising that small commodity production itself generates the capitalist relations it opposes. Marx argued that petty-bourgeois socialism is ultimately reactionary because it seeks to turn back the historical clock rather than advance beyond capitalism. Contemporary analogues include localist, small-business populism and anti-corporate movements that idealise entrepreneurship while opposing monopoly, without questioning commodity production as such.
