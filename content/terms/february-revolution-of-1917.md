The February Revolution of 1917 overthrew the Romanov autocracy and established a Provisional Government in Russia. Sparked by bread riots, strikes, and military mutinies amid World War I devastation, the revolution produced dual power between the bourgeois Provisional Government and workers' and soldiers' soviets.

Lenin emphasised the revolution's character as a bourgeois-democratic revolution accomplished by proletarian and peasant forces, creating conditions for transition to socialist revolution. The Provisional Government's inability to address demands for peace, land, and bread demonstrated the bourgeoisie's incapacity to complete its own revolution. Trotsky's theory of permanent revolution found confirmation in the dynamic whereby democratic tasks could only be fulfilled by proletarian power.
