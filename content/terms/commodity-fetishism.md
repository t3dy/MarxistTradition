---
term: Commodity Fetishism
german: Warenfetischismus
source: "Capital Vol. 1, Ch. 1 Sec. 4"
related: [value-form, commodity, ideology]
traditions:
  value-form: "Fetishism is an objective inversion inherent in commodity production itself; social relations between producers necessarily assume the form of relations between things, and this is not mere illusion but the real mode of appearance of capitalist social relations."
  harvey: "Fetishism is a mystification of underlying class relations; it obscures the exploitation and social labor that lie behind the apparent self-movement of commodities and their prices."
tags: [value theory, ideology, reification]
---

Commodity fetishism describes the process by which the social relations between producers appear as objective properties of the commodities they produce, or as natural relations between things. It is not a subjective error or false consciousness but an objective feature of a society in which private labor is socially validated only through market exchange. Marx draws an analogy with religion, in which the products of the human mind appear as independent beings endowed with autonomous power.
