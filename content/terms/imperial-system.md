The imperial system refers to the structures of political, economic, and military domination through which capitalist powers have historically organised the exploitation of colonised and peripheral territories. Marx analysed colonialism as integral to primitive accumulation, while Lenin theorised imperialism as the highest stage of capitalism driven by finance capital's need for external markets and investment.

Inter-imperial rivalry, the competitive struggle between capitalist states for territorial and economic dominance, produced two world wars and continues to shape geopolitical conflict. Theorists from Bukharin and Luxemburg to contemporary scholars like Callinicos and Harvey have debated whether imperialism is driven primarily by economic logic, geopolitical competition, or the structural requirements of capital accumulation on a global scale.
