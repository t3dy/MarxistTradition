The proletariat is the class of modern wage laborers who, possessing no means of production, must sell their labor power to survive. Marx and Engels identified the proletariat in the *Communist Manifesto* as the revolutionary subject of capitalist society, uniquely positioned to abolish all class distinctions because its emancipation requires the abolition of private property as such. The proletariat is defined not by poverty but by its structural relationship to capital: it produces surplus value while being excluded from ownership of the means of production. Debates persist over the boundaries of the proletariat, particularly regarding the inclusion of service workers, the unemployed, and those engaged in unwaged reproductive labor. The concept has been challenged by theories of the disappearing industrial working class and defended through expanded definitions encompassing all who depend on selling labor power.
