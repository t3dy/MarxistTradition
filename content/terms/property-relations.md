Property relations (*Eigentumsverhältnisse*) refer to the legally and socially enforced relationships governing ownership and control of the means of production. In historical materialism, property relations constitute a central dimension of the relations of production that define each mode of production: communal property in primitive communism, slave-owner property in antiquity, feudal landed property, and bourgeois private property in capitalism. Marx argued that property relations initially correspond to and promote the development of productive forces but eventually become fetters on further development, precipitating social revolution. The concept distinguishes between mere legal title and effective economic control, recognizing that formal property rights can obscure real relations of exploitation. Debates focus on whether nationalization of property suffices to transform property relations or whether deeper changes in social organization are required.
