Wage labour (*Lohnarbeit*) is the social relation in which workers, lacking independent access to the means of production, sell their labor power to capitalists in exchange for a wage. Marx's 1849 pamphlet *Wage-Labour and Capital* established the foundational analysis, demonstrating that the wage conceals an exploitative relationship: workers produce more value than they receive in wages, with the surplus appropriated by the capitalist. The wage form creates the appearance of a free and equal exchange between buyer and seller of labor power, obscuring the underlying compulsion that drives propertyless workers into the labor market. Marx distinguished labor power, the capacity to work that is sold as a commodity, from labor, the actual activity of production. Wage labour is not a transhistorical category but a specifically capitalist social form, arising from the historical separation of producers from the means of production through primitive accumulation.
