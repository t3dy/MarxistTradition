Ideology and consciousness form a central problematic in Marxist theory, addressing how ruling ideas reflect and reproduce class domination. Marx argued in *The German Ideology* that the ideas of the ruling class are the ruling ideas of each epoch, as the class controlling material production also controls mental production.

Gramsci's concept of hegemony reframed ideology as a terrain of struggle. Althusser theorised ideology as material practice operating through Ideological State Apparatuses. Lukács analysed reification as consciousness produced by the commodity form. Debates continue over whether ideology is cognitive distortion, lived experience, or structural effect, and whether class consciousness emerges spontaneously or requires political mediation.
