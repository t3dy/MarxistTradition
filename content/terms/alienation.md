---
term: Alienation
german: Entfremdung
source: "Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts of 1844"
related: [commodity-fetishism, labor-power]
tags: [labor, philosophy, humanism]
---

Alienation describes the estrangement of workers from the products of their labor, from the labor process itself, from their species-being, and from other human beings. Under capitalism, the worker's activity becomes an external, coerced force that belongs to another rather than an expression of free creative capacity. Marx developed this concept most fully in his early writings, though its concerns persist in the mature critique of political economy in transformed form through categories such as commodity fetishism and the real subsumption of labor under capital.
