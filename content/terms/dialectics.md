Dialectics is the method of grasping reality as a process of development driven by internal contradictions rather than as a static collection of fixed things. Marx describes his dialectical method as the direct opposite of Hegel's: whereas Hegel treats the real world as the external manifestation of the Idea, Marx insists that the ideal is nothing other than the material world reflected and translated in the human mind. The dialectical method in *Capital* traces how categories such as commodity, money, and capital develop out of one another through the resolution and reproduction of contradictions.
