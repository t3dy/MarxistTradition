The petty bourgeoisie (Kleinbürgertum) designates the class of small property owners — shopkeepers, artisans, small farmers, and independent professionals — who own their means of production but do not primarily exploit the labour of others. Marx analysed this class extensively in *The Eighteenth Brumaire*, showing how its intermediate position between bourgeoisie and proletariat produced characteristic political vacillation.

The petty bourgeoisie tends toward individualism and localism, opposing both big capital (which threatens to absorb it) and organised labour (which threatens its property). Under conditions of crisis, it can be drawn toward fascism, as the ruined middle classes seek authoritarian solutions. Poulantzas extended the analysis to the 'new petty bourgeoisie' of salaried managers, professionals, and state employees. Contemporary debates address whether the expansion of self-employment, gig work, and small-scale digital entrepreneurship constitutes a new form of petty-bourgeois existence.
