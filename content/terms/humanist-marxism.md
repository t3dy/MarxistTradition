Humanist Marxism places human emancipation, alienation, and species-being at the centre of Marxist thought, drawing on Marx's 1844 Manuscripts. Prominent humanist Marxists include Fromm, the Yugoslav Praxis group, and Schaff, who emphasised Marx's philosophical anthropology against both Stalinist diamat and structuralist anti-humanism.

The tendency was sharply criticised by Althusser, who posited an epistemological break in Marx's work separating early humanist from mature scientific writings. The humanist-structuralist controversy of the 1960s-70s shaped subsequent theoretical developments in Western Marxism and continues to influence discussions of Marxist ethics and political subjectivity.
