Trade unions are workers' organizations formed to collectively bargain with employers over wages, hours, and working conditions. Marx analyzed unions in *Capital* and *Wages, Price and Profit* as necessary defensive organizations of the working class, essential for preventing wages from being driven below the value of labor power. However, he insisted that unions must go beyond their immediate economic functions and engage in political struggle against the wage system itself. Lenin distinguished between trade-union consciousness, limited to economic demands within capitalism, and revolutionary consciousness that grasps the need to overthrow the system. Debates have centered on whether unions are inherently conservative institutions that integrate workers into capitalism or potential vehicles for revolutionary transformation, the significance of union bureaucracy, the challenge of organizing precarious and informal workers, and the declining density of unionization under neoliberalism.
