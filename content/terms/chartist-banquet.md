The Chartist Banquet refers to the political dining events organized by Chartist leaders and sympathizers during the 1840s as venues for radical political discourse, fundraising, and movement solidarity. These banquets functioned as important sites of working-class political culture at a time when public assembly was frequently restricted by the British state. Marx and Engels attended and spoke at several such events, notably in Brussels and London, using them as occasions to build international connections between Chartist leaders and continental socialists. The banquets illustrate the intersection of sociability and political mobilization in early working-class movements.
