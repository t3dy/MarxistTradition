term: Historical Materialism

Historical materialism is the theoretical framework holding that the material conditions of a society's mode of production fundamentally determine its social, political, and intellectual life. It posits that historical change is driven by the development of productive forces and the class struggles arising from contradictions between those forces and the existing relations of production. Rather than treating ideas or consciousness as the motor of history, historical materialism grounds historical explanation in the real processes of social reproduction.
