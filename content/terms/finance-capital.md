Finance capital designates the fusion of banking and industrial capital into a unified economic power. The concept was systematically developed by Rudolf Hilferding in *Finance Capital* (1910), who analysed how the concentration of capital produced large banks that controlled industrial enterprises through credit, shareholding, and interlocking directorates.

Lenin drew on Hilferding to argue that finance capital constituted the economic foundation of imperialism, driving the export of capital, territorial division of the world, and inter-imperialist rivalry. Bukharin further developed the concept in terms of state capitalism and world economy. Debates have centred on whether finance capital represents a specific stage of capitalism or a recurring tendency, and whether contemporary financialisation continues or transforms the classical thesis.
