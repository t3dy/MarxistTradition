Marxist feminism analyses the relationship between capitalism and women's oppression, arguing that gender subordination is structurally linked to class exploitation rather than being an autonomous system. Engels's *The Origin of the Family, Private Property and the State* located women's oppression in the emergence of private property and the patriarchal family.

The domestic labour debate of the 1970s (Dalla Costa, James, Federici) analysed unwaged housework as essential to the reproduction of labour-power and therefore to capital accumulation. Social reproduction theory (Bhattacharya, Vogel) extended this analysis to encompass all the labour — biological, emotional, educational — required to produce and maintain workers. Marxist feminists have debated whether patriarchy constitutes a separate system (dual systems theory) or is internally related to capitalism. Contemporary work addresses care work, the feminisation of labour, reproductive justice, and the relationship between class, race, and gender as co-constituted forms of domination.
