The Spartacist Uprising of January 1919 was an armed insurrection in Berlin led by revolutionary socialists seeking to establish a council republic in Germany following the November Revolution. The Spartacus League, led by Rosa Luxemburg and Karl Liebknecht, had recently formed the Communist Party of Germany (KPD). Though Luxemburg considered the uprising premature, she and Liebknecht supported it out of solidarity with the insurgent workers. The Social Democratic government under Friedrich Ebert deployed the Freikorps, right-wing paramilitary units, to crush the revolt. Both Luxemburg and Liebknecht were captured and murdered on January 15, 1919. The defeat marked a decisive turning point in European revolutionary history, foreclosing the possibility of German workers' councils following the Russian soviet model. It also crystallized the split between revolutionary communism and social democracy into an irreconcilable antagonism with lasting consequences for the German and international working-class movements.
