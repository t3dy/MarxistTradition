Leninism debates concern the theoretical and strategic legacy of Lenin's contributions to Marxism, particularly the vanguard party, democratic centralism, imperialism theory, and the revolutionary seizure of state power. The term 'Marxism-Leninism' was codified by Stalin to denote official Soviet doctrine, generating controversy over whether it faithfully represented Lenin's thought.

Key debates include: the relationship between spontaneity and consciousness in class struggle; whether the vanguard party model is universally applicable or historically specific to Russian conditions; the nature of democratic centralism and its tendency toward bureaucratic degeneration; and the validity of Lenin's theory of imperialism. Lukács, Gramsci, and the council communist tradition offered alternative readings of Lenin, while Althusser sought to recover Lenin's philosophical contributions. Contemporary debates address Leninism's relevance to movements operating outside classical proletarian forms.
