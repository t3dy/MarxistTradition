Historical class alliances refer to the strategic coalitions between different classes that have shaped revolutionary and counter-revolutionary politics. Marx analysed shifting alliances in *The Eighteenth Brumaire*, showing how the bourgeoisie allied with the peasantry against the working class.

Lenin developed the worker-peasant alliance as the strategic foundation of the Russian Revolution. Gramsci theorised hegemonic blocs uniting classes under the leadership of a fundamental class. Trotsky's permanent revolution challenged stagist conceptions. Contemporary debates address alliances between the working class, new social movements, and the peasantry in the Global South.
