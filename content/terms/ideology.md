Ideology refers to the systems of ideas, beliefs, and representations through which human beings understand and relate to their social conditions of existence. In Marx's early formulation, ideology functions like a camera obscura, producing an inverted image of real social relations and masking the material interests that underpin dominant ideas. The concept has been significantly reworked across the Marxist tradition, from Gramsci's connection to hegemony to Althusser's theory of ideology as a material practice with no outside.
