The base-superstructure model describes the relationship between the economic foundation of society (the base, comprising forces and relations of production) and the legal, political, and ideological forms that arise upon it (the superstructure). Marx argues that changes in the economic base tend to transform the superstructure, though the precise nature of this determination has been one of the most debated questions in Marxist theory. The model is best understood not as a rigid architectural metaphor but as an analytical framework for identifying the material conditions that shape and constrain social, political, and cultural life.
