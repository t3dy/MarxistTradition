Matt Walsh's cultural project operates through a theocratic variant of bourgeois ideology. His essentialist interventions on gender — most notably the documentary interrogation of womanhood — deploy natural-law categories to foreclose any historical-materialist analysis of how gender is socially produced and reproduced through specific modes of production. The insistence on fixed biological categories performs the ideological work of naturalizing the patriarchal family form that capitalism requires for the reproduction of labor power.

Walsh's moral panics function as what Marx would recognize as ideological displacement: genuine social crises rooted in material conditions — the precarity of family life under neoliberalism, the commodification of childhood, the alienation of social reproduction — are reframed as cultural degeneracy requiring moral restoration rather than structural transformation. The Daily Wire apparatus packages this reaction as content commodity, extracting surplus value from the very anxieties that capitalist social relations produce. The theocratic inflection distinguishes Walsh from secular conservatives but serves an identical class function.
