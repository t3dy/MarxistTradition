Matt Taibbi's career traces the political economy of independent journalism under platform capitalism. His Rolling Stone work — particularly on Goldman Sachs and the 2008 financial crisis — represented genuine muckraking in the tradition of exposing how finance capital operates. But the collapse of legacy media's business model pushed Taibbi toward Substack, where the journalist becomes an independent commodity producer whose revenue depends on audience loyalty rather than editorial infrastructure. The Twitter Files project, undertaken in collaboration with Elon Musk, completed a transformation from critic of corporate power to defender of a different faction of capital.

The Taibbi trajectory illustrates a structural problem rather than a personal failing. The Substack model rewards polarisation and brand consistency over the slow, expensive work of investigative journalism. A journalist who built his reputation exposing Wall Street discovered that the subscription economy offers more reliable income for culture-war commentary than for financial exposés. His free speech absolutism, once directed at defending dissent against state power, became indistinguishable from defending platform oligarchs against content moderation — revealing how the libertarian framework cannot distinguish between the speech of the powerful and the powerless.
