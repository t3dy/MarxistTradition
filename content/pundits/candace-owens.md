Candace Owens performs a specific ideological function within racial capitalism. Her role is not primarily to persuade Black audiences — her actual demographic skews heavily white and conservative — but to provide white bourgeois conservatism with an alibi against charges of racism. This is tokenism as ideological production: the Black conservative voice is a commodity whose use-value lies precisely in its capacity to delegitimize structural critiques of racial oppression. The "Blexit" campaign exemplified this logic, manufacturing an astroturf movement to sever the connection between racial consciousness and class solidarity.

From a Marxist perspective, Owens's trajectory from progressive social media to conservative media apparatus is clarifying. Her political positions are less convictions than market positioning — she discovered that Black conservatism commands a premium in the attention economy precisely because of its rarity and ideological utility. The ruling class has always cultivated intermediary figures to manage racial contradictions; Owens is the social media iteration of a long historical pattern that Lenin identified in his analysis of how the bourgeoisie co-opts segments of oppressed groups to divide the working class.
