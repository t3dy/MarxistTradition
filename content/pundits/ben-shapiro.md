<!-- Note: Facts don't care about your feelings as positivist ideology; the debate bro as commodity form; Daily Wire as reactionary media capital -->

Ben Shapiro's brand rests on a positivist ideology masquerading as rationality. The slogan "facts don't care about your feelings" performs a double mystification: it presents bourgeois social relations as objective facts while dismissing structural critique as emotional irrationality. This is ideology in its purest Marxian sense — the particular interest of the ruling class dressed in the garb of universal reason. The debate-bro format, with its emphasis on speed and rhetorical domination, is itself a commodity form: intellectual exchange reduced to competitive performance, consumed as content.

The Daily Wire apparatus Shapiro helms functions as a reactionary media capital enterprise, converting ideological production into surplus value through subscriptions, advertising, and merchandise. Adorno's analysis of the culture industry applies with precision: the apparent diversity of Shapiro's content — politics, culture, film — masks a total uniformity of ideological function. Every product reinforces market individualism, naturalizes hierarchy, and forecloses any critique of capitalism as a totality. The audience is interpellated not as citizens or workers but as consumers of a political brand.
