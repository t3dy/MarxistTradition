Jordan Peterson's intellectual project is bourgeois ideology refined to therapeutic form. The injunction to "clean your room" before criticizing the world is a precise inversion of Marx's eleventh thesis on Feuerbach: rather than changing the world, one must endlessly interpret and manage the self. His Jungian framework naturalizes social hierarchy by grounding it in archetypes and evolutionary biology — the lobster hierarchy serving as a particularly transparent attempt to present class domination as biological necessity. This is what Marx called the "naturalization of historically specific social relations."

Peterson's mass appeal reveals the depth of ideological crisis under late capitalism. Workers confronting precarity, alienation, and meaninglessness are offered not structural analysis but individual responsibilization. The self-help commodity — books, lectures, subscription content — transforms existential suffering produced by capitalist relations into a market for personal improvement products. Adorno would recognize this as the culture industry at its most insidious: the very damage wrought by the system becomes raw material for new circuits of accumulation, while the system itself remains unquestioned.
