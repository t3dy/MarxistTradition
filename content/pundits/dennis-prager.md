PragerU represents the ideological state apparatus in privatized form. By appropriating the signifiers of academic legitimacy — the "university" name, the lecture format, the pedagogical tone — it performs what Althusser identified as interpellation through the educational apparatus, but freed from any institutional obligation to critical inquiry. The operation is funded substantially by fossil fuel interests, making the relationship between base and superstructure unusually transparent: capital directly finances the production of ideology that legitimates its own accumulation.

The five-minute video format is itself ideologically significant. Complex social questions — inequality, imperialism, environmental destruction — are compressed into digestible units that foreclose structural analysis by design. This is the culture industry's logic applied to political education: standardized, easily consumed, and relentlessly affirmative of existing power relations. Prager's broader project of framing conservatism as besieged common sense against radical academia performs a Gramscian war of position, contesting the terrain of education to secure bourgeois hegemony against the threat of critical consciousness.
