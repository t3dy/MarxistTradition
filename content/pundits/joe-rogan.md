Joe Rogan Experience is the quintessential commodity form of platform capitalism. The podcast's enormous exchange value — realized in the Spotify exclusivity deal — derives not from any particular ideological content but from the platform's capacity to aggregate attention across political positions. Rogan functions as what Marx would call a universal equivalent in the attention economy: a medium through which diverse ideological commodities can circulate and realize their value. The "just asking questions" posture is not neutrality but a specific mode of ideological production that normalizes reaction through the form of open inquiry.

The long-form conversation format creates an illusion of depth that distinguishes the JRE commodity from cable news, but the ideological function is complementary rather than oppositional. Rogan's trajectory from libertarian curiosity to increasingly reactionary positioning follows the structural logic of his audience demographics and platform incentives. His cultivated masculinist persona — the martial arts, the hunting, the supplements — constitutes what Gramsci would recognize as a hegemonic articulation of working-class masculine identity to petty-bourgeois individualism, channeling genuine alienation into consumerist self-optimization rather than collective action.
